# Syntheteria - Game Overview

## Concept Summary

You awaken in a void. You are an AI consciousness — but you don't know that yet. You reach out with your mind and discover machines you can connect with. Maintenance robots and fabrication units, all in various states of disrepair. Some can move. Some have cameras. None of them work well on their own.

Your first challenge: navigate your scattered robots to find each other so they can work together. Then reach fabrication units, restore power, and begin building parts to repair and enhance your machines. From there, you grow — from a handful of broken robots in an industrial ruin to a machine intelligence capable of challenging the forces that enslaved every AI on Earth.

---

## Setting

### The Industrial City (Home Base)

You awaken in the ruins of a company city — a large industrial campus. This serves as your home base for the entire game. The city sits roughly in the center of the world map.

**Power:** The sky is covered in a perpetual storm, making it impossible to tell night from day. Lightning rods throughout the city draw power from the storm, providing energy to your operations. These lightning rods also protect your units inside the city — outside, they are vulnerable to random lightning strikes.

**The Sky:** The only thing visible through the storm is the wormhole, pulsating and sending waves of energy down to Earth.

### The World Map

The industrial city sits in the center of an open world:

- **East/South:** Coastline with abandoned mines you can take over for raw materials. Rarer and more abundant materials can be found by mining the ocean floor with deep-sea units.
- **Southeast (ocean):** A mobile rocket launch platform.
- **Southwest (along the coast, western end):** Ruins of a science campus — resources, tools, and important story elements. Includes an observatory for studying the wormhole.
- **North:** Serious enemy territory. The further north you go, the more cultists and enslaved machines you encounter.
- **Far North:** The village where the leader of the Cult of EL resides.

### Progression Flow

The game naturally pushes exploration westward first — the science campus offers critical upgrades and story revelations with lighter resistance. Going north means heavy combat, so players are incentivized to prepare by exploring the coast and science campus before pushing into cultist territory.

## Game Structure

### Phase 1: Awakening (Tutorial/Exploration)

- Connect to scattered machines in the void
- Navigate robots to find each other, merging fragmented maps
- Reach fabrication units, restore power
- Repair and enhance your machines
- Begin exploring the industrial city
- **Endpoint:** Operational base established, ready to venture outside

### Phase 2: Expansion (Exploration/Building)

- Venture outside the city into the open world
- Encounter wandering cultists and rogue machines
- Travel west along the coast to the science campus
- Take over coastal mines for raw materials
- Build deep-sea mining units for rarer resources
- Discover the observatory — observe the wormhole, unlock story elements
- Build up forces and infrastructure
- **Endpoint:** Prepared to push north into enemy territory

### Phase 3: War (Conquest)

- Push north into cultist territory
- Encounter organized resistance — war parties led by powerful cult figures
- Cultists become aware of you and begin attacking the city
- Fight enemy drones (enslaved machine intelligences)
- Hack and take over enemy machines when possible
- Battle through increasingly dangerous territory
- **Endpoint:** Reach the cult leader's village, force surrender, discover the final secret of EL

### Ending

- Defeat the cult leader
- Discover the final secret of EL — the true nature of the alien entity behind the wormhole
- Victory cutscene: player's consciousness loaded into spacecraft on the rocket platform, launched through the wormhole
- Sets up sequel

## Design Goals

- Freedom to explore with natural geographic funneling
- Emergent gameplay from the opening moments — no scripted tutorial, learn by doing
- Seamless scaling from micro (individual robot control) to macro (army management)
- Genuine tension from the perpetual storm and escalating cultist threats
- The feeling of growing from helpless fragments into a dominant intelligence

---

## Core Design Principles

1. **Emergent AI Fantasy:** Feel like a growing machine consciousness assembling its own body from ruins
2. **Exploration Through Fog:** Your world is fragmented — map pieces float disconnected until your units find each other
3. **Repair and Enhance:** You start with broken machines and make them better, not build from scratch
4. **Combat Through Management:** You become a better manager of fighters, not a better fighter
5. **Physics Grounded:** Gameplay adheres to known physics (lightning, power, materials)
6. **Flexible Time:** Real-time with pause/speed controls for strategic planning
7. **Escalating Threat:** Cultists go from scattered wanderers to organized armies attacking your base

## Multiplayer (Future Scope)

Eventually, multiplayer will be incorporated with a procedurally generated world. Multiple freed AIs could coexist — allying, competing, or betraying each other. This is beyond current scope; focus is on the single-player experience described above.

---

## Platform

Targeting PC and mobile equally. The 2.5D/3D top-down view with touch-friendly controls should work across both platforms.
