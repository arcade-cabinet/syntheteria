World Design: Floor Textures + POI System

Zones are out. Three approaches to what replaces them — pick what feels right.

A

Uniform Industrial + POIs Only

One dark concrete texture everywhere. Visual interest comes entirely from POIs — glowing deposits, ruins, discoveries. Board reads clean; map is easy to navigate. Max signal-to-noise for POI discovery.

B

Noise-Mixed Textures + POIs

Seeded noise selects from a palette of 4–5 textures per tile (concrete variants + metal plates + gravel). Natural organic variation, no named zones. POIs still float on top. Feels alive and textured — like a real decayed ecumenopolis floor.

C

Clustered Texture Regions + POIs

Noise-based texture clusters — large contiguous patches of metal vs concrete vs gravel, like city blocks or infrastructure sectors. Readable regions without named zone types. POIs mark specific tiles within any region. Closer to the ecumenopolis sector aesthetic.

In all three approaches:
  • Resources (ore/crystal/scrap) are POI entities on tiles — not zone properties
  • Passability is purely elevation-driven (pit = -1 blocked, bridge = +1 passable)
  • Discovery POIs (ruins, signal caches, anomalies) are rare, unique, named like Civ6 wonders
  • TileZone type is removed from TileData entirely
