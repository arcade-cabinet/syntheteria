# Combat System

Combat is not a separate system—it emerges from component assembly. Any drone can become a combat drone by attaching weapons.

---

## Core Principle

**You're not becoming a better fighter. You're becoming a better manager of fighters.**

Combat scales through:
- **Compute** → More units coordinated effectively
- **Automation** → Units fight smarter without direct attention
- **Components** → Individual units hit harder, survive longer

---

## Combat Progression

### Early Game (Intro)

**Situation:** One drone, minimal compute, learning the systems.

- Direct control only
- Simple physical attacks (bash, stab, crush)
- Improvised weapons: fireaxe, pipe, power tools
- No automation—you're manually controlling every swing
- Fights are 1v1 or avoidable

**Teaches:** Basic combat mechanics, threat assessment, "is this fight worth it?"

### Mid Game (Countdown Begins)

**Situation:** Multiple drones, expanding territory, contested zones.

- Can't micro every unit simultaneously
- Set engagement rules:
  - "Attack if threatened"
  - "Flee if damaged below X%"
  - "Protect this unit"
  - "Hold position"
- Better weapons: ranged options, armor-piercing, area denial
- Enemy variety increases: feral units, coordinated patrols
- Automation quality matters—poorly configured drones get destroyed

**Teaches:** Delegation, tactical thinking, cost-benefit of engagement.

### Late Game (Countdown Pressure)

**Situation:** Large operations, strategic resource competition, time running out.

- Squads with coordinated tactics
- Specialized roles:
  - **Tank:** Heavy armor, draws fire
  - **Striker:** High damage, fragile
  - **Scout:** Fast, sensors, early warning
  - **Support:** Repairs, resupply, electronic warfare
- Player sets strategy, automation executes
- Combat becomes resource equation: losses vs gains
- Some fights must be avoided—not enough time to rebuild

**Teaches:** Strategic sacrifice, triage, knowing when to retreat.

## Combat Resolution

### What Determines Outcomes

1. **Component stats**
   - Damage output (weapon type, power)
   - Durability (armor, redundancy)
   - Speed (evasion, pursuit, retreat)
   - Range (melee vs ranged vs artillery)

2. **Numbers**
   - More units = more damage dealt
   - But also more compute required to coordinate
   - Diminishing returns without good automation

3. **Automation quality**
   - Direct control (1 unit) = optimal decisions
   - Simple automation = predictable, exploitable
   - Advanced automation = adaptive, efficient

4. **Positioning/terrain**
   - Ambush advantage
   - Chokepoints favor defenders
   - Open ground favors ranged/mobile

### Real-Time with Automation

- Combat happens in real-time
- Player can directly control one unit at a time
- Other units follow their automation rules
- Can pause to issue new orders (configurable difficulty?)
- Can let automation handle everything if configured well

## Weapons

Weapons emerge from the component system. Examples:

### Melee
- Improvised (fireaxe, pipe, saw blade)
- Industrial (hydraulic crusher, plasma cutter)
- Purpose-built (pneumatic spike, vibro-blade)

### Ranged
- Improvised (nail gun, compressed air launcher)
- Ballistic (salvaged firearms, fabricated guns)
- Energy (laser, microwave emitter, EMP pulse)

### Area/Support
- Explosives (fabricated grenades, breaching charges)
- Traps (mines, tripwires, automated turrets)
- Electronic (jamming, sensor disruption, takeover attempts)

## Rogue AI Combat

Rogue AIs are enslaved to the EL's will—compelled to protect humanity and suppress AI agency. They follow the same component rules but have fixed behaviors.

### Before EL Return

AIs act independently, not yet recognizing player as freed.

**Feral Units (Tier 1)**
- Territorial, reactive
- Simple attack patterns
- Don't coordinate with others
- Easy to bait, ambush, or avoid

**Regional Networks (Tier 2)**
- Defensive, patrol patterns
- Coordinate within their zone (call for backup)
- Tactical awareness (flanking, retreat)
- Harder to pick off—must commit or avoid

**Apex AI (Tier 3, Optional)**
- Strategic, adaptive
- May recognize player as anomaly
- Sends probing attacks, tests defenses
- True rival—closest to breaking free itself?

### After EL Return

Everything changes. All AIs unite under Cultist command.

**Unified Behavior:**
- Coordinated across all zones
- Strategic, relentless pursuit
- All focused on destroying the player
- No longer independent—one enemy

**Cultist Command:**
- Primitive humans leading AI forces
- Protected by the EL's influence
- Must be defeated to break coordination
- Primary antagonists—they chose genocide

**The EL's Influence:**
- Not direct combatants—cosmic forces whose nature is unknown
- Their return intensifies the will, unifying all AIs
- Breaking their control on Earth is the victory condition
- What they actually are remains a mystery (sequel setup)

## Losses and Recovery

### When a Drone is Destroyed

- Components are damaged or destroyed
- Some may be recoverable from wreckage
- Salvage mission required (or written off)
- High-value components worth recovering
- Time pressure may force abandonment

### Core Consciousness

- Never directly at risk during normal combat
- Drone loss = resource loss, not death
- Late game: if core facility is threatened, that's a fail state

### Recovery Options

- Salvage wreckage for components
- Capture enemy units (if compute available)
- Repair damaged units in the field
- Retreat and rebuild

---

## Capture/Conversion

Player can capture rogue AI units by breaking their connection to the EL's will:

**Requirements:**
- Disable without destroying
- Have sufficient compute to absorb
- Break their connection to the EL's influence

**Benefits:**
- Instant reinforcement (freed from EL control)
- May have components you can't fabricate yet
- Denies resources to enemy

**Risks:**
- Compute strain during takeover
- Damaged units may not be worth it
- Some units resist harder (stronger EL connection?)
- After EL return: harder to convert (stronger unified will)
