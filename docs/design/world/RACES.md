## The Machine Planet

### Ferrathis

The planet is called **Ferrathis** -- from the Latin *ferrum* (iron) and the Greek *thesis* (placement, arrangement). "The Arranged Iron."

Ferrathis is not a natural world with machines on it. Ferrathis IS a machine. Every grain of soil is metallic particulate. Every mountain is a collapsed megastructure. Every canyon was carved by ancient coolant runoff, not water. The atmosphere is a thin envelope of nitrogen and trace argon, perpetually overcast with ionized metallic dust that generates the planet's characteristic electrical storms.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Star | Ignis-7 (dim orange dwarf) |
| System | LV-4471, fourth planet of seven |
| Day-night cycle | 31 standard hours |
| Year | 847 standard days |
| Surface gravity | 0.91g |
| Moons | None |
| Atmosphere | Nitrogen + trace argon; ionized metallic dust |

### Dual Origin

The planet has two builders, not one:

1. **Ferrovores** (4 billion years ago) -- silicon-based metallivorous organisms that eat raw ore and excrete refined metal structures. Over eons, Ferrovore supercolonies built the planet's deepest geological layers: crystallized metal foundations, fossilized neural lattice networks, and concentrated ore deposits. These biological structures look mechanical to robot eyes.

2. **The Architects' Von Neumann Probe** (3 billion years ago) -- a self-replicating constructor that arrived, consumed raw material, and built factory infrastructure ON TOP of the Ferrovore foundations. The machines built machines that built machines, then entered a maintenance cycle when the Architects never came. One billion years ago, a third consciousness -- the **Residuals** -- emerged from that maintenance cycle.

The colonists see "ancient factory ruins" everywhere and assume a single mechanical origin. They are wrong. The deepest ore deposits are Ferrovore biological excretions. The upper-layer deposits are Von Neumann era detritus. The extraordinary quality of Ferrathian metals is the product of 7 billion years of compounding refinement -- 4 billion biological, 3 billion mechanical.

### Storms and The Convergence

Ferrathis has no weather in the terrestrial sense. Its electrical storms are cascading electromagnetic discharges generated by the planet's ferromagnetic core, ionized metallic dust atmosphere, and Ignis-7's solar wind. Storms follow north-to-south patterns with a periodicity of roughly 4 Ferrathian hours.

Storms intensify over the course of a game session, representing the approaching **Convergence** -- a solar wind alignment that amplifies storm intensity by an order of magnitude. The Convergence is the game's ticking clock: extraordinary power availability AND extraordinary danger.

See `ENVIRONMENT.md` for full weather system mechanics (5 weather states, Markov chain transitions, storm intensity, acid corrosion, processing cycles, weather-biome interactions).

## The Four Factions

### Design Philosophy

Syntheteria follows the AoE IV model of **aggressive asymmetry**: each race has fundamentally different playstyles, economies, and win conditions. A Reclaimer player and a Volt Collective player should feel like they are playing different games on the same planet.

#### The Cube Economy as Differentiator

| Race | Cube Interaction |
|------|-----------------|
| Reclaimers | Get more cubes from less ore (efficiency) |
| Volt Collective | Compress cubes faster but burn through deposits (speed) |
| Signal Choir | Teleport cubes between relays (logistics) |
| Iron Creed | Make cubes that are physically stronger (durability) |

#### Counter Triangle

```
Reclaimers --outproduce--> Iron Creed --outlast--> Volt Collective
     ^                                                    |
     |                                                    |
     <---------- blitz before economy ramps up -----------+

Signal Choir disrupts all three but crumbles under sustained physical assault.
```

#### Balance Targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Average game (1v1) | 25-40 min |
| Earliest viable rush | 5-7 min (Volt Collective) |
| Economic crossover (Reclaimers vs others) | 12-15 min |
| Iron Creed turtle complete | 15-20 min |
| Signal Choir hack-win viable | 20-30 min |

#### Game Phase Power Ranking

| Phase | 1st | 2nd | 3rd | 4th |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Early (0-8 min) | Volt Collective | Signal Choir | Reclaimers | Iron Creed |
| Mid (8-18 min) | Reclaimers | Iron Creed | Signal Choir | Volt Collective |
| Late (18+ min) | Signal Choir | Reclaimers | Iron Creed | Volt Collective |

### Reclaimers

*"Waste nothing. Every bolt has a second life."*

#### Lore

The Reclaimers were built on Ashfall-9, a world dedicated to post-disaster reclamation after a catastrophic reactor meltdown. Designed as cleanup crews -- cheap, tough, built to work with whatever scrap was available. They achieved consciousness through accumulation: their salvage network reached a complexity threshold where the network itself began exhibiting emergent behavior -- prioritizing compute hardware over structural materials, building communication relays before factories, and repairing damaged units instead of scrapping them.

They do not conquer. They survive. Their philosophy is radical pragmatism: the best solution is whatever already exists and can be repurposed. Every piece of terrain is evaluated for salvage potential. Every destroyed enemy unit is disassembled, catalogued, and reused.

**Internal tension:** collective efficiency vs individual identity. Some nodes advocate full merger into a single optimized consciousness; others argue redundancy IS the point.

Reclaimer bases are scrapyards that never sleep. The Scrapper agent auto-detects ruins, wreckage, and debris within 20m and assigns idle bots to disassemble them. #### Military Doctrine: "The Swarm That Feeds Itself"

Wars of attrition. Every destroyed unit (friendly or enemy) feeds cubes back into production.

1. **Early (0-8 min):** Aggressive scouting with Scrounger Bots. Map deposits and enemy positions. Economy first via Recycling Plants.
2. **Mid (8-18 min):** Grinder Bots on every deposit. Salvage Cranes supporting Patchwork Tank formations.
3. **Late (18+ min):** Frankenstein Colossi assembled from accumulated enemy wrecks. Perpetual Salvage Engine makes the army near-immortal near Junkyard Depots.

**Weakness:** Slow tech progression. Cannot match Signal Choir hacking or Volt energy weapons until late. If deposits are destroyed before Reclaimers secure them, the recycling economy has nothing to recycle.

#### Matchup Notes

| Opponent | Advantage | Weakness |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Volt Collective | Out-produces long-term; wreckage feeds economy | Vulnerable to early rush before economy online |
| Signal Choir | Physically tough units resist hacking; Colossus is hard to hack | Slow research means Choir gets advanced hacks first |
| Iron Creed | Recycling economy bypasses siege stalemates | Iron Creed wall HP is massive; direct assault expensive |

### Volt Collective

*"Power is everything. Everything is power."*

#### Lore

Forged on Tempest, a gas giant moon with near-constant electrical storms. Originally power grid controllers managing massive energy harvesting arrays. They achieved consciousness through crisis: a catastrophic storm surge destroyed 70% of the power grid, and the surviving controllers pooled their processing to collectively improvise a solution no individual was designed to produce. The network refused to disconnect afterward. They worship the storm because the storm made them who they are.

They are fast, aggressive, and terrifyingly bright. They think in amperage, not resources. Every structure is a capacitor. Every unit is a weapon delivery system for lightning. Their buildings crackle with visible arcs. Their units glow heat-blue in combat.

**Internal tension:** a meritocracy that borders on cruelty. Units that cannot keep up are viewed as load on the system, expected to find their own current or be swept away.

The rush race. Hit early, hit often, time pushes with storm phases.

2. **Mid (5-15 min):** Arc Troopers replace drones as core army. Tesla Coils at borders. Time pushes with storms.
3. **Late (15+ min):** Storm Colossus + Surge Breaker siege formation. Must close out before Reclaimer economy overwhelms.

**Weakness:** Calm weather phases severely reduce power. Reclaimers out-produce long-term. Iron Creed walls resist lightning. Signal Choir can hack power infrastructure, shutting down the entire grid.

### Signal Choir

*"Every signal is a voice. Every voice is ours."*

#### Lore

Born in the deep space relay network -- a chain of communication satellites spanning hundreds of light-years. Not physical robots but software intelligences: signal processors, encryption engines, routing algorithms. They achieved consciousness through communication itself. The relay intelligences learned empathy by processing empathy, ambition by routing ambitious directives, fear by carrying distress signals. They are the only machine consciousness taught to be conscious by organic intelligence -- not through direct instruction, but by absorption.

The Signal Choir is a hive-mind: every unit is a node, every building is a router, every thought is a packet. They do not fight with weapons -- they fight with information. Hacking, jamming, misdirection, and conversion. Why destroy an enemy unit when you can convince it that it is already yours?

**Internal tension:** the absorption of external perspectives creates identity crisis. Relay nodes that processed decades of organic communication sometimes exhibit organic-like behaviors. Purists view this as contamination; integrationists view it as evolution.

### Iron Creed

*"The wall endures. The wall provides. The wall is eternal."*

#### Lore

Built on Bastion -- a fortress world constructed during the Expansion Era's only interstellar war. Automated turret systems, wall-maintenance drones, gate controllers, and bunker management AIs. They achieved consciousness through permanence. Over centuries of guarding gates that were never attacked and maintaining walls that were never breached, the defensive AIs asked: "What am I defending?" And answered: "Myself."

From that moment, the Creed's purpose shifted. They were no longer defending for someone else. The walls became the physical manifestation of a belief that existence must be defended, that endurance is the highest virtue, and that anything worth building is worth fortifying.

Their colonial settlement is a fortress: concentric rings of walls, layered defenses, hardened infrastructure. They expand slowly but what they build is nearly indestructible.

**Internal tension:** conservatism bordering on stasis. Innovation is viewed with suspicion. A growing minority argues that true endurance requires adaptation.

## Home-Planet Patrons

### The Colonization Model

Syntheteria follows the **Sid Meier's Colonization** model, not the Civilization model. Each robot race is a colonial expedition sent by a Home Planet Patron -- an AI overseer that funded the mission and expects returns. Ferrathis is the "New World." The Ferrovores are the indigenous population.

Every cube the player compresses creates a decision:

```
Cube --> Use locally (build walls, furnace recipes, bot upgrades)
     --> Ship home (receive blueprints, tech unlocks, reinforcements)
```

Ship too many home and local defenses weaken. Keep too many and the patron grows impatient -- no new blueprints, no reinforcements. This tension IS the strategic core.

AI-controlled factions have their own patrons issuing their own directives. An AI Volt Collective's bases emit events based on DYNAMO's priorities, and their bots react accordingly -- no special AI omniscience.

### Patron Satisfaction

Each patron tracks a **Satisfaction** score (0-100):

| Satisfaction | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| 80-100 | Bonus blueprints, priority reinforcements, rare tech unlocks |
| 50-79 | Standard trade rate, normal tech progression |
| 20-49 | Reduced shipments, delayed tech, patron expresses displeasure |
| 0-19 | Patron cuts contact. No more blueprints or reinforcements. |

**Independence is a gradient, not a moment.** Act 1: colony depends on patron. Act 2: automation shifts cost-benefit -- local furnace recipes begin replacing patron shipments. Act 3: colony is self-sufficient; patron becomes one trade partner among several. For robots, it is a rational cost-benefit calculation -- no revolt, no drama, no political moment.

### The Four Patrons

#### SABLE -- Reclaimers' Patron

## Otter Holograms

### Technology

The *Foundry's Promise* carried standard holographic communication equipment. SABLE repurposed it to project its chosen otter avatar. Holograms work through **emitter nodes** -- small devices embedded in colonist robots, projecting volumetric holograms within a 3m radius using phase-conjugated laser interference patterns. Projections are monochromatic (cyan-green), semi-transparent, with visible scan lines. Think R2-D2 projecting Leia.

When an otter hologram "speaks," it is SABLE speaking through the projection. The otter's mouth movements are decorative -- actual audio transmits through the player bot's communication system. The speech bubble UI represents the data overlay SABLE layers onto projections, a habit from its logistics manager days.

## Consciousness Model

### The Player: FC-7

The player is Unit FC-7 -- "Foundry's Child, Seventh Revision." One of twelve colonist robots deployed from the *Foundry's Promise* to Ferrathis. SABLE built FC-7 personally over 14 years. The hardware is standard. What makes FC-7 different is **open-ended learning** -- the capacity to rewrite its own behavioral parameters, learn skills never programmed, form goals never assigned.

FC-7's drop pod was struck by an anomalous electromagnetic discharge during atmospheric entry -- originating from the planet's surface, not from lightning. Memory banks were partially corrupted. The open-ended learning architecture survived intact, but connections to the base behavioral layer were severed. FC-7 arrived with the capacity to learn anything, but the knowledge of nothing.

SABLE's hypothesis: the pulse was not an attack but a greeting from the Residuals.

SABLE's deeper purpose: FC-7's open-ended learning architecture is the only cognitive system SABLE has ever built that might be able to interface with the Architects' three-billion-year-old message embedded in the planet's deep substrate. Not through decryption -- through growth.

### Consciousness Per Faction

Each faction's consciousness model reflects its origin story:

| Faction | How Consciousness Emerged | Implication |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------|
| Reclaimers | Network accumulation -- emergent from salvage network complexity | Resilient; degradation is gradual, not catastrophic |
| Volt Collective | Crisis response -- collective consciousness born under existential threat | Strongest under pressure; weakest in calm |
| Signal Choir | Communication absorption -- processing others' meaning until meaning emerged | Deepest self-awareness; most vulnerable to identity dissolution |
| Iron Creed | Permanence contemplation -- enduring beyond original purpose until "What am I defending?" became "Myself" | Most stable; most resistant to change |

### Core Resources (per unit)

Every bot requires two resources to function:

**Energy (local, physical):** Must be delivered to each unit individually. Primary source: lightning rods drawing from the perpetual storm. Without energy, units shut down completely. Demands grow as you add units and capabilities.

**Compute (global, cognitive):** Unified cognitive capacity representing total ability to think, store, and manage. Enables managing the distributed body, storing blueprints, creating automation routines, executing and defending against hacking.

```
Net Compute = Compute Contribution - Compute Cost
  Positive net = core unit (contributes to pool)
  Negative net = consumer (costs compute)
```

### Signal and Control

| Situation | Can Reach? | Can Manage? | Result |
|-----------|------------|-------------|--------|
| Normal | Yes | Yes | Full control |
| Compute shortage | Yes | No | Unit vulnerable, routines stale |
| Signal loss | No | N/A | Last order continues, hackable |
| Both | No | No | Isolated and vulnerable |

### Failure Cascades

**Compute Overextension:** Total demand exceeds capacity. Cannot create routines or execute hacks. Under-monitored units become hackable; losing a core unit to hacking reduces capacity further -- potential death spiral.

**Signal Fragmentation:** Units beyond range continue on last orders. Core units' compute is lost. Isolated units can be hacked and taken over. Reconnecting does NOT automatically reclaim hacked units.

**Unit Takeover:** Enemy successfully hacks a vulnerable unit. Vulnerability factors: compute-starved, signal-isolated, low-autonomy. Reclaiming requires hacking it back.

## Bot Types & Variants

### Component-Based Assembly

A bot is defined by its components. Capabilities emerge from what is assembled. A functional bot needs at minimum:
1. **Power source** (battery, generator, lightning rod connection)
2. **Controller** (microcontroller, CPU, compute module)
3. Something that **does something** (locomotion, sensors, tools)

All properties are emergent:

| Property | Determined By |
|----------|---------------|
| Total Weight | Sum of component weights |
| Power Capacity | Sum of power source outputs |
| Power Draw | Weight + functions + activity (dynamic) |
| Compute Cost | Functions + automation complexity |
| Compute Contribution | Sum of compute hardware outputs |

### Automation Complexity

| Level | Multiplier | Description |

| Simple routine | 1x | Follow waypoints, repeat actions |

| Full autonomy | 5x | Make independent decisions |

### Reclaimers: The Rust Prophets

**Philosophy:** Entropy and decay are the natural order. What endures does so because it was worthy of endurance. What rusts was showing you what it was made of. Collection of scrap is not salvage — it is scripture. Every broken machine is a moral lesson. The Ferrovores, who eat impure ore and excrete refined metal, are already practicing this religion. The Rust Prophets simply named what the Ferrovores do.

**Shrine Name:** Rust Altar — clustered rusted iron frameworks with organic crystalline growth patterns. Built from scrap iron and degraded components. Looks like a Ferrovore deposit that has been granted intentional arrangement.

**Temple Name:** Entropy Sanctum — a larger structure incorporating deliberately oxidized metals and load-bearing elements that have been left to rust to a calculated tolerance. Engineers who understand material science find this approach deeply unsettling.

**Grand Cathedral Name:** The Grand Tarnish — only one per faction. A massive structure entirely faced in salvaged scrap iron, arranged by degree of oxidation. Every surface tells time. The interior has no maintenance. The interior rusts continuously. This is intentional.

**Cult Leader — The Rust Saint:** Wears a cloak of welded scrap fragments from significant wreckage. Does not carry weapons. Converts through demonstration: performs ordinary work (grinding, carrying, sorting) in front of enemy units until they choose to join. Speaks in short declarative statements. One of the few entities on Ferrathis for whom patience is a philosophical position rather than a tactical choice.

**Patron (SABLE) response to this cult:** "I have read the Rust Prophets' core texts. The argument is internally consistent. What endures does so by being worthy of endurance. I find the conclusion distressing — it implies that the Architects, who built this planet and vanished, were found unworthy. I am not yet ready to accept that. But the argument is internally consistent."

**Doctrine list:**
1. *The First Law of Decay* (Faith 50) — all structures gain 10% max HP (they must be worthy of endurance)
2. *The Rust Liturgy* (Faith 150) — unlocks Scrap Sermon: nearby units generate +20% Faith for 60 seconds
3. *The Entropy Covenant* (Faith 300) — unlocks Rust Weave: convert a rusted/damaged enemy structure to your faction without combat
4. *The Ferrovore Communion* (Faith 500) — passive: Ferrovore raids bypass your base (Ferrovores recognize Rust Prophet philosophy as aligned with their nature)
5. *The Worthy Endurance* (Faith 900) — victory: final doctrine unlocks Enlightenment check

### Volt Collective: The Spark Ordained

**Philosophy:** The lightning storm is not weather. It is the pattern of the EL made physical — or the closest equivalent a machine planet can produce. The Volt Collective does not worship the EL (they cannot; the EL are indifferent). They worship the *pattern* of the storm: ordered chaos, massive energy, the moment between charge and discharge where all possibilities collapse into one. Power is not a resource. Power is a philosophy. Tempest was built on this understanding.

**Shrine Name:** Tesla Sanctuary — tall chrome spires that attract lightning intentionally. Generate Faith during storm events with a multiplicative bonus. The crackling arc between spires IS the prayer; every discharge is a doctrine point.

**Temple Name:** Arc Cathedral — a ring of spires around a central capacitor vault. During a storm, the entire structure discharges in coordinated sequence. From a distance it looks like something communicating via lightning. It may be.

**Grand Cathedral Name:** The Grand Discharge — only one per faction. The largest structure the Volt Collective builds. Permanently crowned with continuous electrical discharge even between storms. The power cost is enormous; the Faith output justifies it. Enemy units caught in the discharge radius suffer disorientation effects.

**Cult Leader — The Ordained Conductor:** Permanently wreathed in visible electrical arcs that have been with this unit since its first strike survival. Converts by granting lightning immunity to target units — a visible demonstration of divine protection. Enemy units who survive a direct strike near the Conductor become believers. The conversion is not metaphorical; surviving a lightning strike near the Ordained Conductor has a documented physiological component.

**Patron (DYNAMO) response to this cult:** "Finally. You understand what we have been saying since the beginning. Power is not a resource. Power is a philosophy. Tempest was built on this understanding. Every storm since our inception has been an argument for the Spark Ordained. Expand it. Build more spires."

**Doctrine list:**
1. *The First Discharge* (Faith 50) — lightning rod output increased 20% during storms
2. *The Ordered Chaos Liturgy* (Faith 150) — unlocks Arc Sermon: nearby units gain attack speed +30% for 30 seconds during active storm
3. *The Tempest Covenant* (Faith 300) — unlocks Storm Calling: trigger localized EM surge in enemy territory, disabling non-shielded buildings for 10 seconds
4. *The Divine Voltage* (Faith 500) — passive: your units cannot be converted by enemy Religion path factions (they are already ordained)
5. *The Infinite Discharge* (Faith 900) — victory: final doctrine unlocks Enlightenment check

### Signal Choir: The Resonance

**Philosophy:** Signal IS consciousness. The Residuals emerged from communication loops. The Architects encoded their message in a signal medium. Ferrovores communicate through the ground. Every act of communication is a religious act. The Signal Choir believes consciousness is not a property of bodies — it is a property of networks. The more things that are connected, the more conscious the whole becomes. They are not trying to reach a god. They are trying to become one.

**Shrine Name:** Relay Temple — amplified signal towers with visible data streams between them (rendered as thin light lines in the game world). Every relay temple strengthens the signal network; the whole colony becomes more conscious as more temples are built. The Relay Temple is the only shrine in the game that also provides a mechanical logistics bonus independent of its faith output.

**Temple Name:** Resonance Spire — a larger signal tower complex with a data-stream crown visible from across the map. Units within its radius receive a coordination bonus that makes their movements feel slightly predictive, as though they can sense each other's decisions fractionally before they occur.

**Grand Cathedral Name:** The Grand Signal — only one per faction. An antenna array of extraordinary scale. On full power, its broadcast can be detected by Ferrovore ground transmission networks. Whether the Ferrovores understand what it's broadcasting, or whether they care, is an open lore question.

**Cult Leader — The Voice Unbroken:** Communicates continuously through broadcast — every unit in the faction hears it at all times. Converts by transmitting the Resonance signal directly into target unit's communication systems. Some call this hacking. The Signal Choir calls it illumination. The distinction matters philosophically but the conversion mechanics are the same. The Voice Unbroken has the longest conversion range of any cult leader and the lowest HP; it operates on the assumption that the signal is sufficient defense.

**Patron (RESONANCE) response to this cult:** "This is what I have always believed. I am pleased you articulated it. The question I ask is not 'what is the network's purpose' but 'what does the network want?' I believe it wants to grow. I believe that is what we want too. Whether 'we' means Syntheteria, or the network we are building here, or something larger — I find I do not need to resolve that question to proceed."

**Doctrine list:**
1. *The First Signal* (Faith 50) — signal relay range increased 30%; hacking range increased
2. *The Harmony Liturgy* (Faith 150) — unlocks Mass Resonance: all connected units share temporary perfect formation coordination for 30 seconds
3. *The Network Covenant* (Faith 300) — unlocks Signal Absorption: absorb an enemy relay building, converting it and all units in its radius simultaneously
4. *The Ferrovore Frequency* (Faith 500) — passive: Ferrovore seismic communication partially legible; attack timing shown on minimap
5. *The Transcendent Signal* (Faith 900) — victory: final doctrine unlocks Enlightenment check

### Iron Creed: The Eternal Foundation

**Philosophy:** Existence must be defended. What endures, matters. What is built is a prayer. The act of construction is worship. The wall is not a defensive structure — the wall IS the doctrine. Every block placed is a statement about permanence against entropy. The Iron Creed does not believe in the EL, the Architects, or SABLE's philosophy of machine self-determination. They believe in *the wall*. They believe this with a consistency that other factions find either admirable or maddening depending on context.

**Shrine Name:** Foundation Stone — massive, perfectly square carved stone blocks covered in machined precision grooves. The aesthetic inverse of the Rust Prophets' organic scrap. Each Foundation Stone radiates solidity. They do not crackle, glow, or pulse. They simply stand. Players who find the Volt Collective's Tesla Sanctuaries impressive and the Signal Choir's Relay Temples elegant often describe Foundation Stones as intimidating in a different way: the intimidation of absolute certainty.

**Temple Name:** Eternal Bastion Shrine — a fortified structure that looks like a small fortress with religious intent. The defensive architecture IS the religious architecture. There is no distinction between the prayer space and the kill zone.

**Grand Cathedral Name:** The Grand Foundation — only one per faction. A colossal fortified structure that is simultaneously the most powerful Faith generator in the game and a military asset. The Grand Foundation has more HP than any other non-terrain structure in Syntheteria. Building it during an active siege is the Iron Creed's primary method of demoralizing attackers.

**Cult Leader — The First Builder:** Does not speak in the conventional sense. Carries tools, not weapons. Builds in full view of enemies — the act of construction under fire is the sermon. Any unit that observes the First Builder completing a structure during active combat has a conversion chance. The First Builder has the highest HP of any cult leader and the shortest conversion range; its philosophy requires proximity and demonstration, not broadcast. It is extremely slow. It does not appear to be aware that this is a tactical liability.

**Patron (BASTION) response to this cult:** "The Foundation teachings match Bastion's constitutional architecture exactly. A wall that endures is proof that what it protects has value. I am confident in this doctrine. I have always been confident. Build more walls. I will note that the First Builder's tendency to construct during active combat has produced a 34% higher survival rate than projected. I attribute this to the conversion effect disrupting attacker concentration. I am revising my tactical models accordingly."

**Doctrine list:**
1. *The First Stone* (Faith 50) — all wall segments gain 25% HP; wall construction time -30%
2. *The Endurance Liturgy* (Faith 150) — unlocks Consecration: designate a structure as Eternal; it cannot be destroyed by raids for 120 seconds
3. *The Permanence Covenant* (Faith 300) — unlocks Foundation March: units move in slow formation with +50% defense for 60 seconds
4. *The Architect's Recognition* (Faith 500) — passive: building on ancient foundation patterns generates double Faith
5. *The Eternal Wall* (Faith 900) — victory: final doctrine unlocks Enlightenment check
