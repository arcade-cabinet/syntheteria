# Syntheteria — Game Identity

## What Kind of Game Is This?

Syntheteria is an **open-world RTS with 4X exploration and a core storyline**. It's not a campaign with missions. It's not a pure 4X with turns. It's a world handed to the player.

### The RTS DNA
- Real-time with pause and speed controls
- Base building: lightning rods, fabrication units, defensive positions
- Resource management: scavenge, mine, fabricate
- Unit control: select, move, attack, set engagement rules
- Escalating enemy pressure: cult raids increase over time
- You take TIME to fortify before pushing out

### The 4X DNA
- **eXplore:** Fog of war reveals an infinite procedural labyrinth
- **eXpand:** Found bases, claim territory, establish resource chains
- **eXploit:** Scavenge salvage, mine coasts, deep-sea extraction
- **eXterminate:** Push against the Cult of EL, hack enemy machines

### The Storyline
There IS a core narrative spine — discovering what you are (an AI), what EL is (alien entity behind the wormhole), why the cult enslaves machines, and ultimately launching through the wormhole. But:
- **Story is DISCOVERED, not delivered.** You find story beats by exploring.
- **No mission briefings.** No waypoints. No quest markers.
- **Scripted encounters** happen when you stumble on significant places — an observatory, a cult shrine, the leader's stronghold.
- **Dialogue** between you (the AI) and cult figures you encounter.
- **The rest is up to the player.** When to push, when to turtle, what to explore first.

## The Player Experience

### You Start Broken
Two damaged maintenance robots. One has a working camera but broken arms. The other has working arms but a broken camera. Together they can see AND act. Apart, they're nearly useless. Your first minutes are about making them find each other and work together.

### The World Is Handed To You
An infinite procedural labyrinth stretching in all directions. The industrial ecumenopolis — a dead city covering the entire planet. Perpetual storm overhead. Lightning rods power your operations. Everything is ruins. Everything can be scavenged.

### You Grow Organically
- Fix robots → scavenge parts → fabricate components → build better robots
- Establish base → power it → produce units → expand territory
- Explore outward → find resources → encounter enemies → adapt and overcome
- No tech tree menus — you discover what you can build by finding blueprints and salvage

### Difficulty Is Geographic
- Close to spawn: safe, moderate resources, scattered salvage
- Further out: more enemies, better resources, cult patrols
- The coast: mines, deep-sea extraction, moderate danger
- The campus: research discoveries, story revelations, moderate danger
- The north: heavy cult presence, organized resistance, the leader

### The Cult Doesn't Wait
The Cult of EL escalates over time regardless of what you do. They start as wandering patrols. They grow into organized war parties. Eventually they find your base and raid it. This puts time pressure on the player — you can't turtle forever.

## What Makes It Fun (Starcraft/Warcraft Lessons)

1. **Building up feels satisfying.** Establishing a fortified base with lightning rods, fabrication lines, and patrol routes. Looking at your well-organized defense and feeling safe.

2. **Pushing out feels tense.** Every new corridor could have enemies. Every room could have salvage or a cult shrine. The fog of war keeps it mysterious.

3. **Managing your economy is a puzzle.** Power from storm via lightning rods. Salvage from ruins. Components from fabrication. Which robots to repair first? Which to sacrifice?

4. **The cult raids keep you honest.** Can't just explore forever — need defenses. Can't just turtle — need to push eventually. The escalation creates urgency.

5. **Discovery is rewarding.** Finding the observatory. Learning about the wormhole. Encountering a cult figure who speaks to you. These are earned through exploration, not handed to you.

6. **The ending is epic.** Fighting through to the cult leader, defeating them, discovering EL's secret, launching through the wormhole.
