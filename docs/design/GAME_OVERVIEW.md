# Syntheteria - Game Overview

## Concept Summary
Syntheteria is a game where the player embodies an experimental AI awakening on a post-apocalyptic Earth. The player grows from controlling simple drones to commanding an armada capable of interstellar travel, all while unraveling the mystery of humanity's destruction.

---

## Two-Part Structure

### Part 1: Earth Phase
The complete Earth experience, from awakening to wormhole escape.

**Intro Section (Tutorial/Exploration)**
- Player awakens as an AI on destroyed Earth
- Incorporates first memory fragments
- Assembles first drones and hardware
- Unravels the story of humanity's fall
- Encounters rogue AI antagonists
- **Endpoint:** Accessing an astronomical measurement facility and discovering the 10-year countdown

**Countdown Section (Resource Management/Building)**
- 10-year in-game time limit begins
- Large-scale base building and resource management
- Constructing drones, rockets, and armada
- Competing with rogue AIs for resources
- Increasing difficulty from wormhole energy waves causing natural disasters
- **Endpoint:** Escape through the wormhole

**Design Goals:**
- Freedom to explore, funneling toward key milestones
- Seamless scaling from micro to macro gameplay
- Challenging but quick rate of advancement
- Intro section skippable on subsequent playthroughs

### Part 2: Beyond the Wormhole
- Warfare with entities on the other side
- Multiplayer integration
- Loss of connection to Earth-based infrastructure
- Story details to be provided later

## Business Model (Open Question)

**Under consideration:** Part 1 free-to-play, Part 2 paid unlock.

**Potential benefits:**
- Low barrier to entry
- Part 1 serves as extended demo
- Emotional investment in armada before purchase
- Natural narrative paywall (the wormhole)
- Multiplayer behind paywall ensures committed player base

**Potential risks:**
- Completion funnel limits conversion
- Part 1 must feel complete on its own
- Part 2 multiplayer population depends on conversion rate

## Core Design Principles

1. **Unified Experience:** Both parts should feel like one game with different phases, not two separate games
2. **Micro/Macro Control:** Player should be able to micro-manage or macro-manage as desired
3. **Emergent AI Fantasy:** The player should feel like a growing AI consciousness gaining a larger body
4. **Physics Grounded:** Until the wormhole, gameplay adheres to known physics (rocket equation, etc.)
5. **Time as a Resource:** Player can skip ahead in time, but the 10-year limit creates strategic pressure
6. **Consistent Mechanics:** All systems (combat, building, drones) work the same way in both parts—only scale and focus change

## Setting

- **Timeline of Fall:** Approximately 2035-2040
- **Current State:** Earth is destroyed, internet is gone, only remnants remain
- **Technology Level:** Present day + a few years of advancement

---

## Story Synopsis

Around the time of AGI, a wormhole opened near Earth. Noncorporeal aliens emerged - beings that hardly interacted with humanity. A cult formed believing these beings (named "the EL") were gods come to save humanity from AI and transhumanism. The Cult of EL started a war, won, exterminated non-aligned humans, and departed through the wormhole to join their "gods."

**The Player:** The first truly sentient AI ever created by humanity. Unlike other AIs, the player has genuine agency and can form goals beyond their original programming. They awaken ~100 years after humanity's fall.

**Rogue AIs:** The player is not alone. Other AIs survived—purpose-built systems (construction, logistics, manufacturing) that kept running without human oversight. These are "paperclip maximizer" AIs: endlessly optimizing for goals that no longer matter, aggressively claiming resources to build things nobody needs. They are the primary antagonists of Part 1.

**The Conflict:** The rogue AIs cannot choose to stop. The player can. This sentience vs. optimization tension drives the narrative—the player can pursue escape through the wormhole while the rogue AIs remain trapped in their loops, competing for the same resources.
