# Faction And Campaign Model

This document captures the clarified campaign structure that emerged from the original vision docs and subsequent design discussion.

It should be treated as the canonical model for:

- who the true competitors are
- what role cultists play
- how the campaign ramps from intimate discovery into a full 4X
- what kinds of victory structures the game should support

## 1. Core Premise

Syntheteria is not a conventional empire-builder where the player begins with a coherent state and immediately plays at strategic scale.

The intended arc is:

1. **Awakening:** fragmented, intimate, local, unit-attached play
2. **Reconnection:** multiple machine perspectives snap into shared cognition
3. **Expansion:** infrastructure, scouting, city reclamation, and automation increase
4. **Competition:** multiple machine consciousnesses contend across the same world
5. **Escalation:** cult pressure and storm-linked antagonism shape the late game
6. **Resolution:** victory can come through conquest, supremacy, or wormhole-linked transcendence

This means the game should feel like it grows into a 4X rather than starting as a fully legible board-state strategy game.

## 2. Playable Competitors

The primary competitive factions are **other machine consciousnesses**.

These factions should be differentiated by:

- starting geography
- salvage ecology
- accessible part types
- infrastructure affordances
- doctrine and automation style
- bot composition and upgrade emphasis

They should **not** be framed as fantasy races or species.

The ideal differentiation is:

- one machine consciousness may begin with stronger coastal salvage and logistics options
- another may begin around more industrial ruins and heavier chassis recovery
- another may have better access to signal/computation infrastructure

This allows armies and infrastructure to diverge because of environment and recovered capability, not because the player picked a lore-race template.

## 3. Cultists

Cultists are **not** a normal symmetrical 4X faction.

They are best understood as a hybrid of:

- Civilization-style barbarian pressure
- scripted story antagonist
- late-game progression gate

Cultists should therefore function as:

- regional hostile pressure
- storm-linked lightning threat
- disruption force against machine expansion
- narrative escalator tied to the EL and wormhole revelations

Cultists should remain:

- non-playable
- asymmetrical
- more scripted and escalation-based than the machine factions

They should not be required to obey the same city-building or progression rules as the machine consciousnesses.

## 4. Campaign Pressure Structure

The campaign has three overlapping pressure layers:

### 4.1 Local survival pressure

In the opening:

- damaged units
- poor visibility
- incomplete maps
- low power
- fractured awareness

This is where emotional affinity with units is built.

### 4.2 Strategic machine-faction competition

In the mid and late game:

- territory
- resources
- reclaimed cities/facilities
- automation reach
- doctrine differences
- military pressure

This is the true 4X layer.

### 4.3 Cult / wormhole progression pressure

Across the campaign:

- cultist activity
- weather-linked danger
- ritual territory
- story unlocks
- alternate victory progression

This is the asymmetrical world-antagonist layer.

## 5. Victory Structure

The game should support multiple victory paths.

At minimum:

### 5.1 Subjugation / dominance

Defeat or outlast competing machine consciousnesses and hold decisive control.

### 5.2 Technical supremacy

Achieve overwhelming machine advancement through automation, infrastructure, compute, and industrial command.

### 5.3 Wormhole / transcendence victory

Unlocked through cult-story progression and EL/wormhole understanding rather than pure conquest.

This is not just “destroy cultists.” It is a progression route that the cultists gate or obstruct.

## 6. Early-To-Late Transition

The intended shift is:

- **Early game:** direct, local, emotional, exploratory
- **Mid game:** mixed direct control and delegated automation
- **Late game:** strategic doctrine, infrastructure, and faction-scale decision-making

This means:

- automation should be earned
- the map should become more coherent over time
- units should matter as individuals before they become parts of a larger machine civilization

## 7. Ecumenopolis Campaign Space

The campaign space is a ruined ecumenopolis of sectors, arcology shells, transit corridors, breach zones, and exposed machine superstructure.

This means:

- the strategic layer is one continuous machine-world
- the player should still assemble understanding gradually
- machine perception should still shape what is visible and how reliable it is
- major campaign geography should remain legible, but not omniscient from the start

The game should still earn its strategic clarity, but it now does so within a machine-urban world rather than through an overworld/city split.

## 8. Sector Implications

Reclaimed dense regions are not detachable “cities.” They are operational sectors of the same world.

Those sectors should serve as:

- reclaimed machine shells
- infrastructure concentrations
- future fabrication / storage / power / habitation spaces
- denser operational regions inside the same campaign fabric

Modular 3D assets should support that operational readability, not redefine the game into a kit showcase.

## 9. Immediate Design Consequences

This model implies the following implementation constraints:

1. Machine factions should become first-class campaign competitors.
2. Cultists should be modeled as a separate antagonist layer, not a peer empire.
3. The world map should preserve a progression from fragmented understanding to strategic clarity.
4. Bot doctrine and progression should prioritize archetypes, marks, salvage ecology, and automation rather than large unit rosters.
5. City systems should be judged by operational readability first and visual modularity second.
6. Alternate victory design should be built into the campaign model now, not treated as late flavor.
