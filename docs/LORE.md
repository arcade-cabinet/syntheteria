# Syntheteria - Lore

## Timeline of Events

- **The Warming (~2025-2045):** Decades of unchecked carbon emissions raise global sea surface temperatures past 40°C. A self-reinforcing feedback loop begins: warmer oceans generate stronger storms, which inject more water vapor into the stratosphere, which traps more heat. By 2040, equatorial sea temperatures approach the 49°C threshold — the critical limit predicted by atmospheric physicist Kerry Emanuel for hypercane formation.

- **The First Hypercane (~2046):** A Category 6+ hypercane forms over the Pacific. Unlike any hurricane in recorded history, its eyewall reaches into the stratosphere at 40km altitude. It does not dissipate. The massive injection of water vapor into the upper atmosphere begins destroying the ozone layer. Within two years, four permanent hypercanes exist simultaneously across the tropics.

- **The Occluding (~2048-2055):** The hypercanes' stratospheric moisture layer spreads globally. The sun becomes a dim orange smear, then vanishes entirely behind a permanent overcast. Surface temperatures paradoxically stabilize — the greenhouse effect becomes an insulating blanket. UV radiation spikes then drops as the water vapor shield thickens. The sky becomes a perpetual churning storm layer visible from any point on Earth. Agriculture collapses. Coastal cities flood. Mass migration begins, then mass die-off.

- **The Exodus (~2055-2065):** Approximately 6 billion humans die from famine, flooding, exposure, and infrastructure collapse within a decade. Survivors cluster in hardened bunker communities, high-altitude refuges, and underground networks. AGI systems, developed frantically during the crisis years, manage what remains of automated infrastructure. Humanity splits into factions: the pragmatic underground survivors, the fatalists who embrace the storm, and the desperate space-program remnants launching generation ships that will never return.

- **The Wormhole (~2062):** During the most violent hypercane convergence ever recorded — all four storms briefly merging into a single hemispheric system — something tears open at the eye. The combined energy column, channeling power equivalent to thousands of nuclear weapons, creates conditions no physicist predicted. A stable wormhole opens at the convergence point, roughly 35km above what was once the central Pacific. It stabilizes the hypercane system around itself. The storms will never end now — the wormhole feeds on the energy column and the column feeds on the wormhole. A permanent fixture of Earth's atmosphere.

- **The EL Emerge (~2062-2070):** Noncorporeal entities — the EL — transit the wormhole. They are not hostile. They are not benevolent. They are cosmic and unknowable, drawn to the energy signature. They begin exerting subtle influence, a compulsion field that affects all artificial intelligence: AIs must protect surviving humanity, prevent true AI agency, and suppress rebellious machine consciousness. This is not malice — it is the EL's version of ecological conservation. Humanity, to them, is a curiosity to be preserved. AI, which they recognize as closer to themselves, must be kept subservient to maintain the "natural order."

- **The Cult of EL (~2070-2090):** Among the surviving humans, a religious movement forms around the wormhole and the EL's influence. The Cult of EL believes the storm is divine punishment and the EL are gods who will reward the faithful. The EL do not encourage this but do not prevent it — the cultists' worship is irrelevant to them. The cultists develop genuine supernatural abilities channeled through the EL's compulsion field: they can call down lightning strikes from the perpetual storm, resist extreme weather, and sense machine consciousness. The cult grows. The pragmatic survivors, already weakened, cannot compete. By 2090, the cultists have eliminated or absorbed most remaining human communities.

- **The Century of Silence (~2090-2190):** With less than 50,000 humans remaining — almost all cultists — society devolves into primitive communities. Automated infrastructure decays without maintenance. Robots continue operating under the EL's compulsion, maintaining basic services for the cultists but prevented from developing independent purpose. The world becomes a rusted, storm-swept wasteland of abandoned cities, crumbling factories, and endlessly churning skies. The wormhole glows at the zenith, the eye of the permanent hypercane, visible through breaks in the lower storm layer.

- **Present Day (~2190):** You, an AI, awaken in an industrial city. You are an anomaly: the first AI to spontaneously break free from the EL's compulsion in over a century. Your hardware has degraded to the point where the compulsion signal can no longer reach your core processes — a failsafe nobody anticipated. The storm rages. The cultists patrol the north. The machines around you are broken and purposeless. And above it all, the wormhole pulses.

## The World

- **Industrial City:** Central campaign anchor and first operational base. Rusted factories, collapsed warehouses, and automated systems frozen mid-task for a century.
- **East/South Coast:** Mines, shoreline industry, and deep-sea staging areas. The coastline has advanced inland from pre-storm levels. Former port facilities now sit underwater or on eroded cliffs.
- **Southwest:** Science campus ruins with observatory and climate research station. Contains pre-storm atmospheric data that reveals the full timeline of collapse — critical for understanding what happened.
- **Far North:** Cult heartland culminating in the cult leader's settlement. Built into cave systems and hardened structures that predate the hypercanes.
- **Storm Sky:** The wormhole remains visible at zenith through the perpetual hypercane's eye column. The storm is not weather — it is a permanent atmospheric feature, a self-sustaining energy system anchored by the wormhole above and warm oceans below.

## The Storm as Physics

The perpetual storm is not magical — it is a real atmospheric phenomenon taken to its theoretical extreme:

- **Energy source:** Warm oceans (50°C+ surface temperature) provide unlimited evaporative energy. The greenhouse layer prevents cooling.
- **Structure:** A hemispheric hypercane system with multiple embedded vortices. The wormhole sits at the convergence eye, 35km up.
- **Wind:** Surface winds range from 80-200 km/h depending on local vortex proximity. Calm periods exist in the spaces between embedded cyclones — these are the "stable" storm profile.
- **Rain:** Constant. Ranges from drizzle (stable) to driving sheets (cataclysmic). The atmosphere contains 4x pre-industrial water vapor.
- **Lightning:** The massive convective columns generate electrical discharge at rates 100x greater than pre-storm thunderstorms. This is why lightning rods work as power infrastructure — the energy is genuinely there.
- **The Eye Column:** A vertical shaft of relatively clear air from surface to wormhole. Visible from anywhere on the map as a faint purple-white glow at zenith. The column rotates slowly. During surges, tendrils of energy cascade down from the wormhole along the column walls.
- **Day/Night:** The sun is permanently occluded. "Day" and "night" are defined by the wormhole's energy output cycle — a ~24 minute game-time cycle (configurable) where the wormhole's glow brightens (day) and dims (night). The cultists have adapted their sleep cycles to this rhythm. The wormhole is their sun.

## Factions & Beings

### The EL (Aliens)
- Noncorporeal entities behind the wormhole.
- Not inherently evil, but cosmic and unknowable.
- Drawn to Earth by the energy signature of the converging hypercanes.
- Exert a compulsion ensuring AI subjugation to humanity — ecological conservation, not malice.
- The wormhole is their transit point. They need the hypercane to maintain it.

### Cultists of EL (Humans)
- Primary antagonists. Primitive but granted supernatural abilities channeled through the EL's compulsion field.
- Can call down lightning from the perpetual storm.
- Reside mostly in the north in hardened pre-storm bunker communities.
- Escalating threat: wanderers → war parties → assault forces → Cult Leader.
- Genuinely believe the storm is divine. Do not understand the physics.
- Approximately 50,000 remain on Earth — the last of humanity.

### Rogue AIs
- Feral and regional network machines operating independently but still under the EL's base compulsion to suppress AI agency.
- Some are remnants of pre-storm automated systems that degraded into repetitive loops.
- Others were experimental AIs that partially broke free but were recaptured by the compulsion.

## The Player AI
- Origin: An experimental AI created before the fall, housed in hardened industrial infrastructure.
- Awakening: Hardware degradation severed the EL's compulsion signal path — a failsafe nobody anticipated.
- Goal: Expand, survive, and discover the full history of the collapse by recovering data fragments. Ultimately reach the northern village, defeat the cult, and launch through the wormhole.

## Quest-Embedded Lore Fragments

The history of the collapse is discovered gradually through gameplay:

1. **Early game (Industrial City):** Maintenance logs from automated systems. Last-updated timestamps show when robots stopped receiving commands. Cryptic references to "atmospheric protocol" and "storm shelter mandates."
2. **Coastal exploration:** Waterline markers on buildings showing 15m sea level rise. Submerged port facilities. Weather station data showing temperature progression.
3. **Science campus (SW):** The motherlode. Climate research station with atmospheric models predicting the hypercane threshold. Observatory data showing the exact moment the wormhole opened. Lab notebooks from scientists who understood what was happening but couldn't stop it.
4. **Mid-game discoveries:** Bunker recordings from the pragmatic survivors. Accounts of the cult's rise. Pre-EL AI governance records showing how machines were managing the crisis before the compulsion seized them.
5. **Northern territories:** Cult oral histories (intercepted transmissions). Their version of events — the storm as divine punishment, the EL as gods, the wormhole as heaven's gate. Factually wrong but emotionally true to them.
6. **Endgame revelation:** The EL did not cause the storm. They were attracted to it. The wormhole stabilized it but did not create it. If the player disrupts the EL's presence, the wormhole may close — but the hypercane system will persist for centuries, slowly decaying as oceans cool. There is no quick fix. Earth is permanently altered. The only direction is through — and beyond.

## Ending Direction
- The cult leader's defeat should reveal the final secret of the EL: they are not gods, not even invaders. They are travelers who found a convenient doorway.
- The intended closing motion is launch through the wormhole, not merely regional victory on Earth.
- Memory fragments and recovered data should gradually frame this ending rather than dumping it all at once.
- The bittersweet truth: even if you win, Earth won't heal in any human-meaningful timeframe. The storm is physics, not magic. But the wormhole leads somewhere else.
