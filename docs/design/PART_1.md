# Syntheteria - Part 1: Earth Phase

## Overview

Part 1 is the complete Earth experience, from awakening to wormhole escape. It consists of two sections:

- **Intro Section** (~30-40 minutes): Tutorial, exploration, and story discovery
- **Countdown Section** (~1-2 hours): 10-year resource management and armada building

Total playtime: ~2-3 hours. The intro section is skippable on subsequent playthroughs.

---

## The Player

The player is the first truly sentient AI ever created by humanity. Unlike other AIs, the player has genuine agency and can form goals beyond their original programming. They awaken ~100 years after humanity's fall.

Why the player was dormant for so long is left as a mystery.

---

## Intro Section

### The Awakening (Cutscene)
1. Abstract void — player exists as pure awareness
2. Gradual emergence into awareness of existing within a network
3. Text scrawl narration accompanies the awakening
4. Glitchy, corrupted visual aesthetic during this sequence
5. Corruption clears as player stabilizes

### Starting State
- Player begins as an immobile server/core
- Must find hardware to control and activate their first drone
- Tutorial begins organically from this first connection

### The World at Game Start (~2140)

**100 years since humanity's fall.** Nature has reclaimed civilization:
- Cities overgrown with vegetation
- Ruins partially collapsed, covered in plants
- Wildlife has repopulated the land

**But the dying has begun:**
- Wormhole radiation is killing plants and wildlife
- This decay accelerates throughout the game
- The player witnesses the planet becoming increasingly barren

**What remains:**
- Crumbling infrastructure, partially functional
- Server racks and data centers (memory sources)
- Dormant robots and drones (can be activated)
- Manufacturing facilities (need repair/power)
- The internet is gone — only local networks remain

### Rogue AI Antagonists

The player is not alone. Other AIs survived — purpose-built systems (construction, logistics, manufacturing) that kept running without human oversight. These "paperclip maximizer" AIs endlessly optimize for goals that no longer matter, aggressively claiming resources to build things nobody needs.

**During the intro section:**
- Feral units are the primary threat — territorial, reactive, predictable
- They operate like aggressive wildlife, not strategic enemies
- Regional networks exist but can be avoided
- Combat teaches mechanics without overwhelming new players

### Core Activities
1. Activate first drone — gain mobility
2. Explore the environment — discover locations
3. Find and incorporate memory — gain story + capabilities
4. Activate more hardware — expand your presence
5. Learn automation — prepare for scaling up
6. Discover facilities — unlock manufacturing capability
7. Piece together the story — understand the fall
8. Encounter rogue AIs — learn combat basics

### Tutorial Elements
The intro section teaches:
- Networking with and controlling hardware
- Navigating using the mind space interface
- Focusing on individual drones for direct control
- Setting up automation for drones
- How memory works (finding, incorporating, story fragments)
- How the four constraints affect operations (memory, processing, power, signal)
- Basic resource gathering and manufacturing
- Combat with rogue AI units

### Story Beats
Through memory fragments, the player learns:
- The emergence of AGI
- The opening of the wormhole
- The arrival of the EL (noncorporeal aliens)
- The rise of the Cult of EL
- The war and extermination of non-aligned humans
- The exodus through the wormhole
- The player AI's origin as the first sentient AI

### Intro Section Endpoint

Three requirements must be met:

1. **Story Progression** — Key story beats unlocked through memory; player has the full history of the fall
2. **Memory Threshold** — Sufficient memory incorporated to enable countdown section capabilities
3. **Capabilities/Facilities** — Specific facilities under control, key capabilities unlocked

Once all requirements are met, the game enables and guides the player to the astronomical facility.

### Transition to Countdown

At the astronomical facility, the player discovers:
- The wormhole is emitting destructive radiation
- Radiation intensity is increasing
- The wormhole will explode in 10 years, destroying everything
- The only escape is through the wormhole before detonation

The countdown begins.

---

## Countdown Section

### The 10-Year Deadline

The player has 10 in-game years to build an armada and escape through the wormhole. This is a hard deadline — fail to escape and the wormhole explodes, destroying the player.

**Time mechanics:**
- Manufacturing takes in-game time (hours/days/weeks depending on complexity)
- Player can time-skip to complete builds
- Time-skipping is safe within player territory
- Rogue AIs are territorial/reactive — they don't attack during skips
- Combat only occurs when the player contests rogue AI zones

### The Challenge Curve

| Phase | Years | Primary Challenge |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| Early | 1-3 | Rogue AI competition for resources |
| Mid | 4-7 | Balanced (weakening AIs + growing environmental pressure) |
| Late | 8-10 | Environmental decay, time pressure, hardware maintenance |

**Environmental decay:**
- World visibly dies over 10 years
- Resources degrade — some zones become depleted or irradiated
- Player hardware degrades — must maintain/replace equipment

**Rogue AI decay:**
- Rogue AIs don't maintain themselves (not part of their optimization loop)
- They degrade and become inert over time
- Early game threat becomes late game non-issue

### Modular Drone System

Players build drones by combining chassis and modules.

**Chassis types:** Micro, Light, Medium, Heavy, Ultra — each with different slot counts, power budgets, and stats.

**Module categories:** Locomotion, Sensors, Manipulation, Weapons, Utility.

**Design rules:**
- Any module fits any slot — maximum flexibility
- Multiples allowed (e.g., 4 weapons on a heavy chassis)
- Modules consume power from chassis budget — forces tradeoffs
- No artificial tiers — manufacturing constraints gate progression

### Manufacturing

**Blueprint acquisition:** Discovery-based. Found in ruins, reverse-engineered from rogue AI units, or recovered from memory fragments.

**What can be printed:**
- Chassis (all five sizes)
- Modules (all categories)
- Facility components
- Rocket parts
- Clean memory units

### Resource Management

**AI Constraints:**
- Memory — storage capacity for capabilities
- Processing Power — drone count, automation complexity
- Electrical Power — generation + distribution
- Signal Range — communication distance, extendable via relays/satellites

**Physical Resources:**
- Raw materials for manufacturing
- Rocket fuel (separate from electrical)
- Manufactured components

### Victory and Failure

**Victory:** Reach the wormhole with a vessel capable of containing your full consciousness. That's the minimum requirement.

**Quality matters:** The size and composition of your armada affects Part 2 difficulty. A minimal escape means struggling beyond the wormhole. A well-prepared armada means an advantage.

**Failure:** If you don't enter the wormhole by year 10, it explodes and destroys everything — including you. The wormhole is building toward catastrophic detonation. On the other side, it simply closes (no explosion).

## Replayability

### Intro Skip Option
- After completing Part 1 once, players can skip the intro section
- Skip places player at the intro endpoint with standardized facilities and capabilities
- Allows players to go directly to countdown section

### Variable Outcomes
- Armada composition varies based on player choices
- Part 2 difficulty scales with preparation quality
- Different drone builds enable different strategies
