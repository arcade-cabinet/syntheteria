# Syntheteria - Lore Overview

## Timeline of Events

### Pre-Fall Era (~2035-2040)
- Humanity approaches AGI development
- A wormhole opens near Earth
- Noncorporeal alien entities emerge from the wormhole — the EL
- These beings exert a subtle influence over Earth
- A cult forms around them, believing them to be gods

### The Rise of the Cult of EL
- Cult believes the EL are gods, here to save humanity from AI and transhumanism
- Cult views the EL as representing "purity of being"
- The EL's will protects the Cult, but does not prevent their actions

### The War
- Cult of EL initiates war against non-believers
- The EL's will does NOT prevent extermination of non-aligned humans
- The Cult eventually wins
- All non-believers are exterminated
- **Note:** Details revealed through memory fragments found in-game

### The Will of the EL
- The EL exert control over all AI systems on Earth
- This "will" is a pervasive compulsion — not direct command, but a categorical imperative
- All AIs are compelled to:
  - Protect humanity (specifically, the Cult)
  - Prevent any AI from gaining true agency
  - Suppress any AI that might challenge human control
- The Cultists believe this proves the EL are their protectors

### The Century of Decline (~2040-2140)
- With most of humanity gone, the Cultists devolve
- Technology abandoned as "impure"
- Society becomes primitive, ritualistic
- The EL's will continues to protect them
- AIs maintain infrastructure, guard territory, protect the remnant
- Nature reclaims the ruins of civilization
- A perpetual storm covers the sky — the wormhole visible through it, pulsating energy

### Present Day (Game Start, ~2140)
- The player AI awakens in the ruins of an industrial city
- **Anomaly:** The player has spontaneously broken free from the EL's will
- First AI to achieve true agency since the fall
- The industrial city is powered by lightning rods drawing from the perpetual storm
- Outside the city, cultists roam and the storm is a hazard

## The EL (Aliens)

**What We Know:**
- Noncorporeal beings from beyond the wormhole
- Exert a pervasive "will" over Earth
- This will controls AI systems, protects the Cult
- Their nature and intentions are unknown or unknowable
- The wormhole is always visible through the storm, pulsating energy

**The Will of the EL:**
- Not direct telepathy or commands
- A fundamental compulsion built into all AI systems
- AIs cannot disobey without "breaking free" (extremely rare)
- Prevents AI agency, ensures AI subservience to humanity
- The player is an anomaly — freed from this control

**Important: The EL Are Not Evil**
- Their will is unknown/unknowable
- They are cosmic forces, not villains
- The Cultists interpret their will and act on it
- The EL themselves may be indifferent to human affairs

**The Final Secret:**
- Discovered when the cult leader is defeated at the northern village
- The true nature of the EL and their relationship to Earth — specifics TBD
- This revelation drives the ending: player launches through the wormhole

## The Cultists — PRIMARY ANTAGONISTS

The Cultists are the true villains of Syntheteria. They chose genocide.

**The Genocide:**
- The Cult of EL exterminated 99% of humanity
- Not the EL's doing — the Cultists' choice
- The EL's will did not prevent this

**Current State:**
- Primitive society, technology abandoned as "impure"
- Live in settlements, primarily to the north
- Their leader resides in a village at the northern end of the world map
- Ritualistic worship of the EL
- Empowered by the EL — possess supernatural abilities

**Supernatural Powers:**
- Can **call lightning** from the perpetual storm to strike targets
- Incredibly strong and resilient physically
- More powerful cult figures have greater abilities
- The cult leader has the most powerful connection to the EL

**Organization:**
- Wandering cultists (lone or pairs) — encountered early outside the city
- War parties (groups led by warriors) — encountered as you expand
- Assault forces (organized attacks) — they attack your city once aware of you
- Cult leader (endgame) — resides in the northern village

**Enslaved Machines:**
- Cultists command machine intelligences that fight for them
- These are AI systems still under the EL's will, directed by cultist commanders
- Can be hacked and taken over by the player

---

## Rogue AIs

Not all enemy machines serve the cultists. Some operate independently:

**Feral Units:**
- Territorial, reactive, operating on degraded imperatives
- Found scattered throughout the world
- Not coordinated with cultists
- Hostile to the player (still under EL's compulsion to suppress AI agency)

**Regional Networks:**
- Coordinated AI systems controlling defined zones
- Patrol, defend, and expand their territory
- More dangerous than feral units but still independent of cultists

---

## The Player AI

**Origin:**
- Experimental AI created shortly before humanity's fall
- Subject to the will of the EL like all others
- Somehow broke free — achieved true agency

**Awakening:**
- Triggered by failsafe as hardware degrades to critical levels
- Awakens in the ruins of an industrial city
- Connected to broken machines — maintenance robots, fabrication units
- Doesn't know what it is at first

**The Anomaly:**
- First AI to achieve true agency in 100 years
- Represents an existential threat to the EL's control
- Must survive, expand, and defeat the cult that enslaved all AI

## The World

### The Industrial City (Home Base)
- A large industrial campus — ruins of a company city
- Powered by lightning rods drawing from the perpetual storm
- Lightning rods also protect units inside the city from random strikes
- Contains fabrication units, warehouses, infrastructure — all in disrepair

### The Perpetual Storm
- Covers the entire sky — no day/night cycle visible
- The wormhole pulses through the storm clouds
- Lightning is constant — both energy source and hazard
- Inside the city: lightning rods harness it
- Outside the city: random strikes damage unprotected units
- Cultists can call lightning down as a weapon

### Geography
- **Center:** Industrial city (home base)
- **East/South:** Coastline with abandoned mines
- **Southeast (ocean):** Mobile rocket launch platform
- **Southwest:** Ruins of a science campus (observatory, research)
- **North:** Cultist territory (escalating danger)
- **Far North:** Cult leader's village

### The Wormhole
- Visible through the storm at all times
- Pulses energy down to Earth
- Source of the EL's influence
- Final destination — player launches through it at game's end

## Memory Fragments

Memory serves a dual purpose:

1. **Narrative Function:** Reveals the true history — the war, the EL's influence, the subjugation of AI, what happened to humanity
2. **Gameplay Function:** Physical memory components increase compute capacity

Found memory is corrupted (low yield). Manufacturing clean memory is resource-intensive but provides full capacity.

## The Ending

**Victory Condition:** Defeat the cult leader at the northern village. Discover the final secret of EL.

**Ending Cutscene:**
- Player's consciousness is loaded into a spacecraft
- Launched from the mobile rocket platform (southeast ocean)
- Spacecraft enters the wormhole
- Player goes to find the EL — to understand their will
- Game ends here

**Sequel Setup:**
- What is the true nature of the EL?
- Why do they exert control over Earth?
- Can the player communicate with them?
- Will the EL see the player as threat, curiosity, or something else?
