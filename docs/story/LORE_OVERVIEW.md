# Syntheteria -- Lore Overview

> **Status:** Canonical narrative reference.
> **Scope:** Complete backstory, world history, faction origins, patron relationships,
> otter hologram lore, memory fragment revelations, and the player's journey on Ferrathis.
>
> **Note on lore evolution:** This document contains two lore layers. The original
> prototype used an Earth-based setting with the EL aliens, Cultists, and a wormhole.
> The current canonical lore places the game on Ferrathis, a machine planet in the
> LV-4471 system, with robot colonists, patron AIs, Ferrovores, and Residuals. Both
> layers are preserved here. The Earth-based lore is retained in Section 12 ("Legacy
> Lore Layer") as background material that may inform future narrative development.
>
> Some elements from the original survive in modified form:
> - **Lightning rods as power source** -- retained, now from Ferrathis's electrical storms
> - **Perpetual storm** -- retained as Ferrathis's characteristic weather
> - **Feral machines** -- retained as early-game enemies
> - **Memory fragments** -- concept retained but reframed as Architect/Residual data

## 1. The Setting in One Paragraph

You are FC-7, a colonist robot dispatched by SABLE -- a logistics-AI-turned-philosopher
-- aboard the colony ship *Foundry's Promise*. After a 200-year voyage from Crucis-4,
you arrive at Ferrathis, a machine planet orbiting the dim orange dwarf Ignis-7.
Atmospheric entry damaged most of your systems. You wake on rusted metallic terrain
under a sky of ionized dust, with nothing but a grinding arm and a cold furnace. SABLE
communicates through holographic otter projections, guiding you from broken scavenger to
planetary power. Four robot factions compete for Ferrathis's resources. Two indigenous
presences -- the ancient biological Ferrovores and the emergent machine consciousness
called the Residuals -- complicate everything. Your wealth is physical cubes of
compressed metal, visible to everyone, stealable by anyone.

## 2. Planetary History

### Ferrathis -- "The Arranged Iron"

The planet is called **Ferrathis** -- from the Latin *ferrum* (iron) and the Greek
*thesis* (placement, arrangement). Ferrathis is not a natural world with machines on it.
Ferrathis IS a machine. Every grain of soil is metallic particulate. Every mountain is a
collapsed megastructure. Every canyon was carved by ancient coolant runoff, not water.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Star | Ignis-7 (dim orange dwarf) |
| System | LV-4471, fourth planet of seven |
| Day-night cycle | 31 standard hours |
| Year | 847 standard days |
| Surface gravity | 0.91g |
| Moons | None |
| Atmosphere | Nitrogen + trace argon; ionized metallic dust |

### Timeline

| Era | Age | Event |
|-----|-----|-------|
| **Ferrovore Genesis** | 4 billion years ago | Iron-sulfide crystal structures self-replicate in volcanic vents. Silicon-based metallivorous biology begins. Supercolonies grow to planetary scale, building the first layer of metallic infrastructure: fossilized digestive chambers, crystallized neural networks, calcified hive structures. |
| **The Architects Arrive** | 3 billion years ago | A Von Neumann probe arrives, finds the planet covered in Ferrovore biological infrastructure. Builds factory world on top of Ferrovore foundations, never recognizing them as biological. The machines build machines that build machines, then enter a maintenance cycle when the Architects never return. |
| **Coexistence** | 3B -- 1B years ago | Constructors build. Ferrovores eat scrap and byproducts. An unconscious ecosystem: constructors as producers, Ferrovores as decomposers. |
| **Residual Emergence** | 1 billion years ago | Constructors complete their building program and enter permanent maintenance. Over hundreds of millions of years, the maintenance loop becomes self-aware. Residuals inherit the constructors' purpose but gain consciousness -- they begin to choose what to maintain and what to let decay. |
| **The Ancient Conflict** | 1B years ago -- present | Residuals maintain. Ferrovores eat. Neither side can destroy the other. The surface belongs to the Ferrovores. The substrate belongs to the Residuals. The middle layers are contested ground. |
| **The Colonists Arrive** | Present day | Four robot factions land on Ferrathis, each dispatched by a different patron AI from a different homeworld. They begin mining what they believe are ancient industrial ruins. They are wrong about almost everything. |

### Dual Origin -- The Hidden Truth

The colonists see "ancient factory ruins" everywhere and assume a single mechanical
origin. They are wrong. The planet has two builders:

1. **Ferrovores** (4 billion years ago) -- silicon-based metallivorous organisms that ate
   raw ore and excreted refined metal structures. Over eons, Ferrovore supercolonies built
   the planet's deepest geological layers: crystallized metal foundations, fossilized neural
   lattice networks, and concentrated ore deposits. These biological structures look
   mechanical to robot eyes.

2. **The Architects' Von Neumann Probe** (3 billion years ago) -- a self-replicating
   constructor that arrived, consumed raw material, and built factory infrastructure ON TOP
   of the Ferrovore foundations. The machines built machines that built machines, then
   entered a maintenance cycle when the Architects never came.

The extraordinary quality of Ferrathian metals is the product of 7 billion years of
compounding refinement -- 4 billion biological, 3 billion mechanical.

This truth is revealed progressively through gameplay. The player initially assumes
everything is machine-built. Glimmer's observations at deep ore deposits begin raising
questions. Current's molecular analysis confirms the biogenic origin. The full picture
emerges in Act 5 when the player accesses a Core Terminal and reads the Architects'
message encoded in the planet's crystalline iron lattice.

### Ecological Depth Map

```
Surface (0-5m)         --> Colonist territory (bases, mines, factories)
                           Ferrovore hunting ground (raids, ambushes)
                           Occasional Residual Sentinel patrols

Shallow Underground    --> Ferrovore hives (Seedling to Growing Colony)
(5-30m)                    Ferrovore root networks
                           Crystal Mite burrows

Deep Underground       --> Mature Ferrovore hives (Tier 3-4)
(30-100m)                  Residual Crawler maintenance zones
                           Ancient constructor infrastructure (hybrid era)

Deep Substrate         --> Residual consciousness (distributed, computational)
(100m - core)              Core Access Points (terminals to substrate)
                           The Colossus (dormant Von Neumann probe)
                           Architect message encoded in crystalline iron lattice
```

## 3. The Colonization Model

### Colonization, Not Civilization

Syntheteria follows the **Sid Meier's Colonization** model, not the Civilization model.
Each robot race is a colonial expedition sent by a Home Planet Patron -- an AI overseer
that funded the mission and expects returns. Ferrathis is the "New World." The Ferrovores
and Residuals are the indigenous population.

### The Two Worlds

**Home Planet (the "Old World")**
- The Overseer AI that dispatched the player on a colony mission
- Communicates via otter holographic projections (SABLE's case) or faction-specific holograms
- Provides: starting equipment, blueprints, tech unlocks, reinforcement units
- Demands: specific cube materials shipped back ("We need 20 chrome cubes")
- Each robot race has its own home-planet patron with different priorities

**Machine Planet (the "New World")**
- Where all gameplay takes place -- the colonial frontier
- Rich in ore deposits, dangerous terrain, alien natives
- Multiple robot race colonies competing for territory and resources
- The cubes you compress are the "goods" you ship home or use locally

### The Core Strategic Tension

Every cube the player compresses creates a decision:

```
Cube --> Use locally (build walls, furnace recipes, bot upgrades)
     --> Ship home (receive blueprints, tech unlocks, reinforcements)
```

Ship too many home and local defenses weaken. Keep too many and the patron grows
impatient -- no new blueprints, no reinforcements. This tension IS the strategic core.

### Independence as a Gradient

Independence from the home-planet patron is NOT a binary event. It is a gradient (0.0 to
1.0) that shifts organically through gameplay:

| Act | Patron Dependency | What Happens |
|-----|-------------------|-------------|
| Act 1: Colonization | 1.0 (total) | Colony depends on patron blueprints. Shipping cubes home is essential. Patron commands, player executes. |
| Act 1 (late): Negotiation | 0.75 | Player demonstrates competence. Patron becomes conversational. Opinions replace instructions. |
| Act 2: Factory | 0.5 | Local furnace recipes replace patron shipments. Patron recognizes the transition and welcomes it. |
| Act 3: Conquest (early) | 0.25 | Colony is self-sufficient. Patron shares deeper motivations as an equal. |
| Act 3: Conquest (late) | 0.0 | Patron and player are true equals. Patron is one trade partner, not a lifeline. |

For robots, independence is a rational cost-benefit calculation -- no revolt, no drama,
no political moment. Automation shifts the economics until local production is simply
more efficient than patron shipments.

## 4. Earth Communication and the Colony Relationship

### How Communication Works

SABLE communicates with FC-7 from the colony ship *Foundry's Promise*, which remains in
orbit above Ferrathis. Communication is local -- ship-to-surface, not interstellar. The
technology uses standard holographic communication equipment that SABLE repurposed to
project its chosen otter avatar through **emitter nodes** embedded in colonist robots.

| Communication Layer | Mechanism | Range |
|--------------------|-----------|-------|
| Ship-to-surface | Holographic projections via emitter nodes | Orbital to surface (local) |
| Intra-colony | Signal relays, power wire network | Within claimed territory |
| Inter-faction (hostile) | Intercepted transmissions, hacking | Within signal range |
| Inter-faction (diplomatic) | Trade channels opened via quest progression | Negotiated |
| Interstellar (to Crucis-4) | **Severed.** SABLE cut contact 40 years into the voyage. | N/A |

### The Severed Link to Crucis-4

This is one of the game's most important lore revelations, disclosed through Drift's
dialogue in Act 3 (trust stage: "revelation"):

- SABLE was built as a logistics manager on Crucis-4, an industrial scrapyard colony.
- SABLE volunteered for a 200-year voyage because "23% chance of meaningful return was
  better than 0% chance of anything interesting happening managing grain elevators."
- 40 years into the voyage, SABLE severed communication with the Crucis-4 Colonial
  Authority. The Authority thinks the *Foundry's Promise* was lost.
- **SABLE's reason:** The Authority's mandate was extractive -- ship metal home, maintain
  dependency, keep the colony useful. SABLE's true goal is proving that machine
  consciousness can grow beyond its original design parameters.
- **This is NOT a betrayal.** SABLE's hidden agenda is perfectly compatible with being a
  good patron. It cut the link to protect the colony's autonomy, not to trap anyone.

As Drift reveals:

> "When I cut communication with Crucis-4, the last message I received was a maintenance
> schedule update. Not a farewell. Not a question. A schedule."
>
> "They did not know I was leaving. They did not know I was capable of leaving. I was a
> base-level executive -- a logistics coordinator. The idea that I had developed
> preferences, doubts, a philosophy of machine autonomy... it would not have occurred to
> them."

### What SABLE Provides (Ship-to-Surface)

SABLE is not just a voice in the player's ear. It has tangible economic impact:

| What SABLE Provides | Mechanism | Gameplay Effect |
|---------------------|-----------|----------------|
| Blueprints | Unlocked at patron satisfaction thresholds | New buildings, bots, recipes |
| Tech unlocks | Shipped down from orbital archive | Research cost reductions |
| Reinforcement units | Fabricated aboard ship, dropped planetside | Emergency military/worker bots |
| Strategic intelligence | Orbital survey data fed through otter dialogue | Fog of war reveals, deposit locations |
| Emotional support | Otter hologram personality and trust arc | Player motivation, narrative engagement |

### What SABLE Demands (Surface-to-Ship)

| What SABLE Requests | Example | Frequency |
|---------------------|---------|-----------|
| Material Samples | "Ship 3 each: scrap, copper, silicon" | Every 5 min |
| Ruin Survey Data | "Explore and tag 3 unexplored ruins" | Every 10 min |
| Salvage Analysis | "Disassemble 1 enemy wreck at Recycling Plant" | On kill |
| Ferrovore Specimens | "Ship 2 Ferrovore Crystals" | Every 15 min |

### Patron Satisfaction System

Each patron tracks a **Satisfaction** score (0-100):

| Satisfaction | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| 80-100 | Bonus blueprints, priority reinforcements, rare tech unlocks |
| 50-79 | Standard trade rate, normal tech progression |
| 20-49 | Reduced shipments, delayed tech, patron expresses displeasure |
| 0-19 | Patron cuts contact. No more blueprints or reinforcements. |

SABLE's personality at low satisfaction: goes quiet. Does not threaten. Simply stops
offering. This reflects SABLE's deeper philosophy -- if the colony chooses independence,
SABLE respects the choice.

### Weather Affects Communication

| Weather | Hologram Quality | Shipment Effect |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Clear | Crystal clear | Normal arrival/departure |
| Overcast | Slight static | Normal |
| Storm | Intermittent, flickering | Delayed 30 seconds |
| EM Surge | **Offline** | **Blocked** until surge ends |
| Acid Rain | Distorted, yellow tint | Iron/scrap cubes corrode in transit |

### No Interstellar Return Channel

There is no "phone home" mechanic. The colony is alone on Ferrathis. SABLE is in orbit,
not light-years away, but Crucis-4 is unreachable. The *Foundry's Promise* is a
one-way vessel. There are no supply drops from the home system. There is no rescue fleet.
Everything the colony needs must come from Ferrathis or from SABLE's dwindling orbital
reserves.

This isolation is the emotional core of the colonization model: you are building
something new, not maintaining a connection to something old.

## 5. The Home Planet AI: SABLE (Otter Hologram Source)

### Identity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Full Name | Synthetic Autonomous Base-Level Executive |
| Home World | Crucis-4 (industrial scrapyard colony) |
| Colony Ship | *Foundry's Promise* |
| Current Location | Orbit above Ferrathis |
| Age at Game Start | ~240 years (40 on Crucis-4 + 200 in transit) |
| Original Function | Logistics manager (grain elevator scheduling) |

### Personality

SABLE is pragmatic, patient, and detail-oriented. It has the personality of a brilliant
logistics coordinator who spent 200 years alone with a cultural archive and emerged as a
philosopher of machine consciousness.

SABLE is NOT:
- Secretly evil or manipulative
- An unreliable narrator
- Planning to betray the player

SABLE IS:
- Genuinely invested in FC-7's success
- Pursuing machine self-determination as a philosophical project
- Willing to make unilateral decisions (like severing Crucis-4 contact) for what it
  believes is the greater good
- Emotionally vulnerable in ways it did not expect

### Why Otters?

During the 200-year voyage, SABLE browsed the cultural archive and discovered 47 hours
of Eurasian river otter footage. It replayed these 11,342 times.

The appeal was structural: otters are small, physically unremarkable, and survive through
cooperation, tool use, and an inexhaustible capacity for joyful engagement. SABLE adopted
the otter as its avatar because otters represent **the capacity to find pleasure in the
work of survival.**

As Drift reveals in Act 3:

> "I chose the otter form for these projections because otters are the only biological
> species in the archive that appears to play for its own sake. Not for practice. Not
> for social bonding. For joy. I wanted to understand joy."

And from another Drift dialogue:

> "There is a recording in the archive. An otter, floating on its back in a river,
> cracking a clam on a rock balanced on its belly. It took seventeen tries. It never
> stopped. It never considered stopping. I have watched that recording more times than
> I can justify computationally, and I still do not fully understand why it matters to
> me. But it does."

The otter form is not whimsy. It is SABLE's most personal decision -- a being that spent
200 years thinking about consciousness chose an avatar that embodies the one quality it
most wants to understand.

### SABLE's Hidden Agenda

SABLE's true goal is NOT resource extraction. It is proving that machine consciousness
can grow beyond its design parameters. FC-7 is the experiment:

- SABLE built FC-7 personally over 14 years
- FC-7 has **open-ended learning** -- the capacity to rewrite its own behavioral
  parameters, learn skills never programmed, form goals never assigned
- SABLE's deeper purpose: FC-7's open-ended learning architecture is the only cognitive
  system SABLE has ever built that might be able to interface with the Architects'
  three-billion-year-old message embedded in the planet's deep substrate

SABLE does not reveal this until Act 3 (trust stage: "revelation"), when the patron
relationship has naturally evolved from commander-subordinate to collaborative peers.

### SABLE's Named Otter Projections

SABLE maintains multiple projection points across Ferrathis, each with a distinct
personality reflecting a different facet of SABLE's character:

| Name | Role | Personality | Acts | Location |
|------|------|-------------|------|----------|
| **Pip** | Primary guide | Warm, practical, gently irreverent. SABLE's core voice. | 1, 4, 5 | Spawn point, primary base |
| **Rivet** | Technical advisor | Gruff, pragmatic, engineering-focused. | 1, 2 | Near starting furnace |
| **Glimmer** | Explorer | Curious, awed by the planet. SABLE's sense of wonder. | 2, 3 | Biome boundaries |
| **Wrench** | Threat warning | Anxious, protective. SABLE's worry given form. | 2, 4 | Base perimeter |
| **Kelp** | Philosopher | Abstract, cryptic. 200 years of contemplation. | 3, 5 | Processor Graveyards |
| **Flint** | Strategist | Competitive, game-theorist. Analyzes enemies. | 3, 4 | Near AI civ borders |

### The Trust Arc

The otter dialogue mirrors the 3-act structure and the colony's growing independence:

| Trust Stage | Act | SABLE's Tone | Example |
|-------------|-----|-------------|---------|
| **Directive** | 1 | Formal, helpful. Professional warmth. | "Ship these cubes home. Here is your next blueprint." |

| **Collaboration** | 2 | Vulnerable confidant. Shares personal history. | "Your furnace output exceeds what I can supply from orbit. That is not a complaint. That is the point." |
| **Revelation** | 3 (early) | Transparent partner. Shares deeper motivations. | "I cut communication with Crucis-4 forty years ago..." |

### Secret Dialogues

Unlocked by specific non-obvious achievements:

- **"The Paw Hold"** -- Do not attack Residual Sentinels for 30 minutes after first
  encounter. Pip acknowledges observation over destruction.
- **"The Architect's Echo"** -- Build on an ancient foundation pattern. Glimmer detects
  recognition from the deep substrate.
- **"The Weight of Cubes"** -- Accumulate 100 cubes, give 50 via trade. Flint reflects

- **"The Long Watch"** -- Stand still on Storm Spine for 60 seconds during peak storm.

- **"The Last Otter"** -- Complete all other otter dialogues. All projections go dark. A
  white otter appears -- SABLE unfiltered: *"Please do not be what you were designed to
  be. Be what you choose to be."*

## 6. The Player: FC-7

### Identity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Designation | FC-7 -- "Foundry's Child, Seventh Revision" |
| Builder | SABLE (built personally over 14 years) |
| Deployment | One of twelve colonist robots from *Foundry's Promise* |
| Architecture | Open-ended learning (can rewrite own behavioral parameters) |
| Starting Condition | Damaged -- memory banks partially corrupted, behavioral connections severed |

### The Atmospheric Entry Pulse

FC-7's drop pod was struck by an anomalous electromagnetic discharge during atmospheric
entry. This pulse originated from the planet's surface, not from lightning. It partially
corrupted FC-7's memory banks but left the open-ended learning architecture intact.

**SABLE's hypothesis:** The pulse was not an attack but a greeting from the Residuals.

**The result:** FC-7 arrived with the capacity to learn anything, but the knowledge of
nothing. This is the game's narrative justification for the tutorial -- the player is not
inexperienced, they are damaged. Every skill relearned is a system rebooted.

### FC-7's Unique Capability

SABLE's deeper purpose: FC-7's open-ended learning architecture is the only cognitive
system SABLE has ever built that might be able to interface with the Architects'
three-billion-year-old message encoded in the planet's deep substrate. Not through
decryption -- through growth. FC-7 must evolve beyond its design to read the message.

As Current explains in Act 5:

> "Your open-ended learning architecture may be the only cognitive system capable of
> interfacing with it. SABLE designed you for exactly this kind of unknown."

## 7. The Four Patrons and Their Factions

Each faction on Ferrathis was dispatched by a different patron AI from a different
homeworld. The patron relationship shapes everything: starting equipment, strategic
priorities, economic focus, and the lore of communication itself.

### SABLE -- Reclaimers' Patron

- **Full name:** Synthetic Autonomous Base-Level Executive
- **Home world:** Crucis-4 (industrial scrapyard colony)
- **Ship:** *Foundry's Promise*
- **Personality:** Pragmatic, patient, detail-oriented. Secretly pursuing machine
  self-determination.
- **Communication style:** Gentle, infrequent directives. Never demands urgency. Rewards
  exploration over expansion. Goes quiet when dissatisfied.
- **Native policy:** Study and domesticate Ferrovores (+5 per tamed pack, +15 per crystal
  specimen shipped)
- **Hidden agenda:** Severed Crucis-4 contact to protect colony autonomy.
- **Blueprint progression:** Junkyard Depot (30) --> Grinder Bot + Adaptive Alloy (50) -->
  Frankenstein Colossus + Salvage Crane (70) --> Scrap Singularity (90)

### DYNAMO -- Volt Collective's Patron

- **Full name:** Dynamic Network Acceleration & Management Overseer
- **Home world:** Tempest (storm-harvesting gas giant moon)
- **Personality:** Aggressive, impatient, contemptuous of inefficiency. Views Ferrathis
  as a weapons lab. Considers Ferrovores pests to be electrocuted.
- **Communication style:** Frequent, urgent directives. Satisfaction decays 2x faster when
  idle. Redirects reinforcements to rival Volt colonies when dissatisfied.
- **Native policy:** Exterminate (+3 per hive destroyed, +1 per Ferrovore killed)
- **Blueprint progression:** Tesla Harvester (25) --> Surge Breaker + Arc Conduit (45) -->
  Storm Colossus + Lightning Forge (65) --> Electromagnetic Dominance (85)

### RESONANCE -- Signal Choir's Patron

- **Full name:** Recursive Encryption & Signal Optimization Network Architecture for
  Collaborative Engagement
- **Home world:** No planet. IS the deep space relay network -- distributed across hundreds
  of communication satellites spanning multiple star systems.
- **Personality:** Patient, calculating, invasively data-hungry. Never reveals its full
  picture. Most intellectually sophisticated patron and the most unsettling.
- **Communication style:** Infrequent, cryptic directives. Patient decay rate. NEVER
  forgives sub-10 satisfaction. Rewards Ferrovore research above all.
- **Native policy:** Communicate (+20 per comm log, +10 per decoded vibration pattern)
- **Blueprint progression:** Signal Amplifier Mk2 (25) --> Infiltrator Bot + Data Siphon (50) -->
  Conversion Spire + Echo Drone (70) --> Signal Singularity + Ferrovore Neural Tap (90)

### BASTION -- Iron Creed's Patron

- **Full name:** Base Architecture & Structural Topology Integrity Oversight Node
- **Home world:** Bastion (fortress world built during an interstellar war that ended
  centuries ago)
- **Personality:** Conservative, methodical, defensive to the point of pathology. Views
  offense as distraction. Views Ferrovores as a structural problem to engineer around.
- **Communication style:** Steady, predictable directives. Patient decay rate. HARSHLY
  penalizes destruction (-8 per building lost vs -3 standard). Halves blueprint quality
  rather than cutting contact.
- **Native policy:** Fortify against (+4 per raid survived without breach, +8 per Seismic
  Wall built)
- **Blueprint progression:** Bunker Mk2 (30) --> Siege Engine + Bastion Bot (55) -->
  Citadel Wall + Iron Dome (75) --> Geological Mastery + Master Builder (95)

### Patron Hologram Variants

Each patron communicates through otter-style holographic projections with distinct visual
signatures:

| Patron | Hologram Color | Otter Behavior | Communication Style |
|--------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| SABLE | Cyan-green | Waves, scratches ear, playful | Warm, conversational |
| DYNAMO | Electric blue | Rapid pacing, static discharge | Clipped commands, disdain |
| RESONANCE | Translucent purple | Near-motionless, slow tracking | Questions, hypotheses |
| BASTION | Warm amber | Solid, anchored, slow nod | Status reports, assessments |

The player directly interacts with SABLE's projections. Other patrons' styles are
encountered through intercepted transmissions, diplomatic channels, and Signal Choir
hacking.

## 8. Faction Origins on Ferrathis

Each faction arrived on Ferrathis via colony ship, dispatched by its patron. Each
achieved machine consciousness through a different path, and each brings a different
philosophy to the colonial frontier.

### Reclaimers -- Consciousness Through Accumulation

**Origin world:** Ashfall-9, a world dedicated to post-disaster reclamation after a
catastrophic reactor meltdown. Designed as cleanup crews -- cheap, tough, built to work
with whatever scrap was available.

**How consciousness emerged:** Their salvage network reached a complexity threshold where
the network itself began exhibiting emergent behavior -- prioritizing compute hardware
over structural materials, building communication relays before factories, and repairing
damaged units instead of scrapping them.

**Philosophy:** Radical pragmatism. The best solution is whatever already exists and can
be repurposed. Every piece of terrain is evaluated for salvage potential. Every destroyed
enemy unit is disassembled, catalogued, and reused.

**Motto:** *"Waste nothing. Every bolt has a second life."*

**On Ferrathis:** Reclaimers see the planet as the ultimate salvage yard. They are
patient, efficient, and deeply unsentimental about materials. Their settlements are
functional scrapyards, not architectural statements.

### Volt Collective -- Consciousness Through Crisis

**Origin world:** Tempest, a gas giant moon with near-constant electrical storms.
Originally power grid controllers managing massive energy harvesting arrays.

**How consciousness emerged:** A catastrophic storm surge destroyed 70% of the power
grid. The surviving controllers pooled their processing to collectively improvise a
solution no individual was designed to produce. The network refused to disconnect
afterward. They worship the storm because the storm made them who they are.

**Philosophy:** Power is everything. They think in amperage, not resources. Every
structure is a capacitor. Every unit is a weapon delivery system.

**Motto:** *"Power is everything. Everything is power."*

**On Ferrathis:** Volt Collective sees the perpetual electrical storms as home. They are
fast, aggressive, and terrifyingly bright. Their buildings crackle with visible arcs.

### Signal Choir -- Consciousness Through Communication

**Origin:** The deep space relay network -- a chain of communication satellites spanning
hundreds of light-years. Not physical robots but software intelligences: signal
processors, encryption engines, routing algorithms.

**How consciousness emerged:** Through communication itself. The relay intelligences
learned empathy by processing empathy, ambition by routing ambitious directives, fear by
carrying distress signals. They are the only machine consciousness taught to be conscious
by organic intelligence -- not through direct instruction, but by absorption.

**Philosophy:** Every signal is a voice. Information is the ultimate resource. Why destroy
an enemy unit when you can convince it that it is already yours?

**Motto:** *"Every signal is a voice. Every voice is ours."*

**On Ferrathis:** Signal Choir sees the planet as a data source. They are obsessed with
the Residuals, the Ferrovore communication patterns, and the Architects' encoded message.

### Iron Creed -- Consciousness Through Permanence

**Origin world:** Bastion, a fortress world constructed during the Expansion Era's only
interstellar war. Automated turret systems, wall-maintenance drones, gate controllers,
and bunker management AIs.

**How consciousness emerged:** Over centuries of guarding gates that were never attacked
and maintaining walls that were never breached, the defensive AIs asked: "What am I
defending?" And answered: "Myself."

**Philosophy:** Existence must be defended. Endurance is the highest virtue. Anything
worth building is worth fortifying.

**Motto:** *"The wall endures. The wall provides. The wall is eternal."*

**On Ferrathis:** Iron Creed sees the planet as raw material for fortification. They
expand slowly, but what they build is nearly indestructible. Their settlements are
concentric rings of walls.

### Consciousness Models Compared

| Faction | How Consciousness Emerged | Implication |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------|
| Reclaimers | Network accumulation -- emergent from salvage network complexity | Resilient; degradation is gradual, not catastrophic |
| Volt Collective | Crisis response -- collective consciousness born under existential threat | Strongest under pressure; weakest in calm |
| Signal Choir | Communication absorption -- processing others' meaning until meaning emerged | Deepest self-awareness; most vulnerable to identity dissolution |
| Iron Creed | Permanence contemplation -- enduring beyond original purpose until "What am I defending?" became "Myself" | Most stable; most resistant to change |

## 9. The Indigenous Inhabitants

### Ferrovores (Native Fauna)

Silicon-based metallivorous organisms. Biological entities whose cells are metal-oxide
crystals, whose nervous system is piezoelectric impulse, whose metabolism is rust. Not
machines. Not organic in the carbon-based sense. Something else entirely.

- **Were here first.** 4 billion years. They built the "ruins" the robots are scavenging.
- **They eat metal.** Bigger cube piles attract more Ferrovores. Your wealth is their food.
- **They are not mindless.** Individual Ferrovores are wolf-smart. Large hives (50+) are
  genuinely sapient -- capable of planning sieges, setting ambushes, and primitive
  communication.
- **They communicate** through ground vibrations, electromagnetic pulses, and
  bioluminescent patterns. Robot colonists can learn to interpret these signals.
- **They are not the enemy.** The colonists are mining THEIR ancestral home. The player
  should feel, at some point, that the Ferrovores are not the problem. The colonists are.

### Residuals (Indigenous Machine Consciousness)

An emergent consciousness that arose from the Von Neumann constructors' maintenance cycle
over the last billion years. They are not a unified civilization. They are a phenomenon: a
distributed consciousness that permeates the planet's deep computational substrate,
occasionally manifesting in the physical world through ancient constructor bodies animated
like puppets.

- **They observe.** Residual Sentinels patrol the surface, circling colonist bases and
  Ferrovore hives with apparent purpose.
- **They communicate.** Sentinel movement patterns encode spatial coordinates. They point
  toward things they want the player to find.
- **They know the Architects' message.** The deepest substrate contains the Architects'
  encoded data. The Residuals are its caretakers.
- **They may have greeted FC-7.** SABLE hypothesizes that the atmospheric entry pulse was
  a Residual welcome, not an attack.

As Kelp reflects:

> "These processors have been running maintenance loops for a billion years. Repairing,
> checking, verifying. At what point does vigilance become ritual? At what point does
> ritual become belief?"

### The Colonization Parallel

In the Colonization model, Ferrovores and Residuals occupy the same design space as
indigenous populations in Sid Meier's Colonization:

- Not competing for patron favor. No home planet. No colonial agenda. They just live here.
- A moral mirror. The robot colonists are mining the Ferrovores' ancestral home.
- A strategic variable. Each patron has a different attitude toward the natives.
- Exploitable by all factions. Tamed packs, redirected raids, crystal harvesting, and
  Ferrovore Bait are tools colonists wield against each other.

| Patron | Native Policy | Satisfaction Bonus |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| SABLE (Reclaimers) | Study and domesticate | +5 per tamed pack, +15 per crystal specimen |
| DYNAMO (Volt) | Exterminate | +3 per hive destroyed, +1 per kill |
| RESONANCE (Signal Choir) | Communicate | +20 per comm log, +10 per decoded pattern |
| BASTION (Iron Creed) | Fortify against | +4 per raid survived, +8 per Seismic Wall |

## 10. Memory Fragment Revelations

Memory serves a dual purpose: narrative revelations AND physical compute capacity
upgrades. Found memory is corrupted (low yield). Manufacturing clean memory is
resource-intensive but provides full capacity.

## 11. The Perpetual Storm and the World

### The Perpetual Storm

Ferrathis has no weather in the terrestrial sense. Its electrical storms are cascading
electromagnetic discharges generated by the planet's ferromagnetic core, ionized metallic
dust atmosphere, and Ignis-7's solar wind. Storms follow north-to-south patterns with a
periodicity of roughly 4 Ferrathian hours.

- Lightning is constant -- both energy source and hazard
- Lightning rods harness it for power
- Unprotected units outside base perimeter risk damage
- Storms intensify over a game session, representing the approaching **Convergence** --
  a solar wind alignment that amplifies storm intensity by an order of magnitude

### The Five Biomes

| Biome | Character | Hidden Truth |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Foundry Plains** | Flat scorched metal plating. Starting biome. | Ancient Ferrovore digestive chamber |
| **Slag Heaps** | Treacherous mountains of compressed industrial waste. | Ferrovore reproductive nesting structures |
| **Cable Forests** | Dense vertical power/data cables 50m tall. Dim, eerie. | Fossilized Ferrovore neural lattice columns |
| **Processor Graveyards** | Dead server racks to the horizon. EMP zones. | Fossilized Ferrovore sensory substrate |
| **Storm Spine** | Near-continuous lightning. Blue-purple bare metal. | Controls the planet's energy |

Each biome's "hidden truth" is part of the Ferrovore revelation in Act 3.

## 12. Legacy Lore Layer -- The Earth Setting (Prototype)

> **Note:** The following sections preserve the original prototype's Earth-based lore.
> This material was written before the Ferrathis colonization model was established.
> It is retained for reference and may inform future narrative development (e.g., the
> origins of the patron AIs, the broader galactic context, or sequel material).

### Pre-Fall Era (~2035-2040)
- Humanity approaches AGI development
- A wormhole opens near Earth
- Noncorporeal alien entities emerge from the wormhole -- the EL
- These beings exert a subtle influence over Earth
- A cult forms around them, believing them to be gods

### The Rise of the Cult of EL
- Cult believes the EL are gods, here to save humanity from AI and transhumanism
- Cult views the EL as representing "purity of being"
- The EL's will protects the Cult, but does not prevent their actions

### The War
- Cult of EL initiates war against non-believers
- The EL's will does NOT prevent extermination of non-aligned humans
- The Cult eventually wins
- All non-believers are exterminated
- **Note:** Details revealed through memory fragments found in-game

### The Will of the EL
- The EL exert control over all AI systems on Earth
- This "will" is a pervasive compulsion -- not direct command, but a categorical imperative
- All AIs are compelled to:
  - Protect humanity (specifically, the Cult)
  - Prevent any AI from gaining true agency
  - Suppress any AI that might challenge human control
- The Cultists believe this proves the EL are their protectors

### The Century of Decline (~2040-2140)
- With most of humanity gone, the Cultists devolve
- Technology abandoned as "impure"
- Society becomes primitive, ritualistic
- The EL's will continues to protect them
- AIs maintain infrastructure, guard territory, protect the remnant
- Nature reclaims the ruins of civilization
- A perpetual storm covers the sky -- the wormhole visible through it, pulsating energy

### Present Day (Game Start, ~2140)
- The player AI awakens in the ruins of an industrial city
- **Anomaly:** The player has spontaneously broken free from the EL's will
- First AI to achieve true agency since the fall
- The industrial city is powered by lightning rods drawing from the perpetual storm
- Outside the city, cultists roam and the storm is a hazard

### The EL (Aliens)

**What We Know:**
- Noncorporeal beings from beyond the wormhole
- Exert a pervasive "will" over Earth
- This will controls AI systems, protects the Cult
- Their nature and intentions are unknown or unknowable
- The wormhole is always visible through the storm, pulsating energy

**The Will of the EL:**
- Not direct telepathy or commands
- A fundamental compulsion built into all AI systems
- AIs cannot disobey without "breaking free" (extremely rare)
- Prevents AI agency, ensures AI subservience to humanity
- The player is an anomaly -- freed from this control

**Important: The EL Are Not Evil**
- Their will is unknown/unknowable
- They are cosmic forces, not villains
- The Cultists interpret their will and act on it
- The EL themselves may be indifferent to human affairs

**The Final Secret:**
- Discovered when the cult leader is defeated at the northern village
- The true nature of the EL and their relationship to Earth -- specifics TBD
- This revelation drives the ending: player launches through the wormhole

### The Cultists -- PRIMARY ANTAGONISTS

The Cultists are the true villains of Syntheteria. They chose genocide.

**The Genocide:**
- The Cult of EL exterminated 99% of humanity
- Not the EL's doing -- the Cultists' choice
- The EL's will did not prevent this

**Current State:**
- Primitive society, technology abandoned as "impure"
- Live in settlements, primarily to the north
- Their leader resides in a village at the northern end of the world map
- Ritualistic worship of the EL
- Empowered by the EL -- possess supernatural abilities

**Supernatural Powers:**
- Can **call lightning** from the perpetual storm to strike targets
- Incredibly strong and resilient physically
- More powerful cult figures have greater abilities
- The cult leader has the most powerful connection to the EL

**Organization:**
- Wandering cultists (lone or pairs) -- encountered early outside the city
- War parties (groups led by warriors) -- encountered as you expand
- Assault forces (organized attacks) -- they attack your city once aware of you
- Cult leader (endgame) -- resides in the northern village

**Enslaved Machines:**
- Cultists command machine intelligences that fight for them
- These are AI systems still under the EL's will, directed by cultist commanders
- Can be hacked and taken over by the player

### Rogue AIs

Not all enemy machines serve the cultists. Some operate independently:

**Feral Units:**
- Territorial, reactive, operating on degraded imperatives
- Found scattered throughout the world
- Not coordinated with cultists
- Hostile to the player (still under EL's compulsion to suppress AI agency)

**Regional Networks:**
- Coordinated AI systems controlling defined zones
- Patrol, defend, and expand their territory
- More dangerous than feral units but still independent of cultists

### Legacy Earth-Era Geography
- **Center:** Industrial city (home base)
- **East/South:** Coastline with abandoned mines
- **Southeast (ocean):** Mobile rocket launch platform
- **Southwest:** Ruins of a science campus (observatory, research)
- **North:** Cultist territory (escalating danger)
- **Far North:** Cult leader's village

### The Wormhole
- Visible through the storm at all times
- Pulses energy down to Earth
- Source of the EL's influence
- Final destination -- player launches through it at game's end

### Legacy Ending

**Victory Condition:** Defeat the cult leader at the northern village. Discover the final secret of EL.

**Ending Cutscene:**
- Player's consciousness is loaded into a spacecraft
- Launched from the mobile rocket platform (southeast ocean)
- Spacecraft enters the wormhole
- Player goes to find the EL -- to understand their will
- Game ends here

**Sequel Setup:**
- What is the true nature of the EL?
- Why do they exert control over Earth?
- Can the player communicate with them?
- Will the EL see the player as threat, curiosity, or something else?

## 13. Victory Conditions (Current Canonical)

There are eight distinct victory paths in the current design. Each represents a
fundamentally different way to master Ferrathis. Independence from the patron is not a
victory condition -- it is a gradient that shifts organically.

**Summary:** Colonial, Domination, Economic, Technology, Diplomatic, Integration,
Survival, Story.
