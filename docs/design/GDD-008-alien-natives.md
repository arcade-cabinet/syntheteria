# GDD-008: The Ferrovores -- Indigenous Population of the Machine Planet

**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Scope:** Complete design for the Ferrovores, the machine planet's indigenous lifeform. In the Colonization model (see GDD-007 Section 1.5, COLONIZATION-MODEL.md), the Ferrovores are the "native population" -- they were here billions of years before the robot colonists arrived. They are NOT competing for patron favor, they have NO home planet, and they are NOT colonists. They are the beings whose planet is being colonized.

---

## 1. Concept: Living Metal

### 1.1 What Are the Ferrovores?

The Ferrovores are **silicon-based metallivorous organisms** -- living creatures made of crystallized metal lattices that *eat* refined metal. They are not machines. They are not organic in the way carbon-based life is. They are something else entirely: biological entities whose cells are metal-oxide crystals, whose nervous system is piezoelectric impulse, whose metabolism is rust.

They were here billions of years before the colony ship arrived. The planet's deepest geological formations -- what the colonists mistake for ancient industrial ruins -- are actually **Ferrovore architecture**. Like coral builds reefs from calcium carbonate, Ferrovores build structures from processed metal. The Von Neumann constructors that arrived 3 billion years ago built their factory infrastructure *on top of* Ferrovore foundations, creating a hybrid planet: biological metalwork at the deepest layers, mechanical construction above. The "ruins" the robot colonists are scavenging are a fusion of both -- and the colonists can't tell the difference.

The robot colonists are mining the Ferrovores' home. They are also mining on top of the Residuals' maintenance domain (see Section 15 for the unified ecosystem).

### 1.2 Why This Design?

| Design Goal | How Ferrovores Achieve It |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Feel alien to mechanical robots | Silicon crystal biology, not metal construction |
| Were here first | Built the "ruins" the robots are scavenging |
| Scale with player wealth | They eat metal -- bigger cube piles = more attractive food source |
| Spawn from unexplored territory | Hives are in deep/unexplored zones |
| Not just combat obstacles | Can be negotiated with, studied, domesticated |
| Visually distinct from all robot races | Organic crystalline forms, bioluminescence, no panels/bolts/chrome |
| Colonization model: indigenous population | No patron, no home planet, no colonial agenda -- they ARE the "New World" |

### 1.3 The Core Tension

The Ferrovores don't want to destroy the robot colonists. They want to **eat their cubes**. A pile of perfectly compressed, refined metal cubes is an irresistible feast to a Ferrovore. This creates the game's most important emergent behavior: **your wealth attracts predators**. The more cubes you have, the more Ferrovores come to feed.

This is the RimWorld wealth-scaling mechanic made physical and thematic. In RimWorld, raiders attack bigger colonies because "they heard about your wealth." In Syntheteria, Ferrovores attack bigger cube piles because they can *smell* the refined metal.

### 1.4 The Colonization Parallel

In the Colonization model, the Ferrovores occupy the same design space as indigenous populations in Sid Meier's Colonization. They are:

- **Not competing for patron favor.** Ferrovores have no Home Planet Patron. They have no colonial agenda. They just live here.
- **A moral mirror.** The robot colonists are mining the Ferrovores' ancestral home -- the "ruins" they scavenge are Ferrovore architecture. This creates an implicit ethical tension without forcing the player into a moral choice.
- **A strategic variable, not a strategic opponent.** Each robot race's patron has a different attitude toward the Ferrovores. The Archivist (Reclaimers) wants specimen data. The Dynamo (Volt) wants them exterminated. The Resonance (Signal Choir) wants to decode their communication. The Architect (Iron Creed) wants them walled out. These patron attitudes shape each race's Ferrovore strategy.
- **Exploitable by all factions.** Ferrovore Crystals, Ferrovore Bait, tamed Ferrovore packs, and redirected raids are all tools that colonists can use against each other. The indigenous population becomes a resource -- uncomfortable, thematic, and mechanically deep.

| Patron | Ferrovore Policy | Satisfaction Bonus |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| The Archivist (Reclaimers) | Study and domesticate | +5 per tamed pack, +15 per crystal specimen shipped |
| The Dynamo (Volt) | Exterminate | +3 per hive destroyed, +1 per Ferrovore killed |
| The Resonance (Signal Choir) | Communicate | +20 per Ferrovore communication log, +10 per decoded vibration pattern |
| The Architect (Iron Creed) | Fortify against | +4 per raid survived without wall breach, +8 per Seismic Wall built |

## 2. Lore

### 2.1 Origin

Four billion years ago, the planet that would become "the machine planet" had a reducing atmosphere rich in iron oxide, silicon, and trace metals. In the volcanic vents of its young oceans, self-replicating iron-sulfide crystal structures emerged -- the ancestors of the Ferrovores. Where Earth's biology built on carbon chains and water solvents, this planet's biology built on metal lattices and silicate frameworks.

Over a billion years, Ferrovore supercolonies grew to planetary scale. They consumed raw ore, processed it through their crystalline bodies, and excreted refined metal structures. Layer upon layer, generation upon generation, the Ferrovores built the planet's *first* infrastructure layer. The deepest geological formations the colonists discover -- ancient foundations beneath the "foundry plains," fossilized neural networks in the "cable forests" -- are Ferrovore architecture, not mechanical construction.

Then the Architects' Von Neumann probe arrived, 3 billion years ago. The constructors did not destroy the Ferrovores. They built on top of them, using Ferrovore crystalline foundations as the base layer for their factory world. The "processor graveyards" are Von Neumann era. The "foundry plains" are a fusion: Ferrovore digestive chambers repurposed as industrial floors. The upper layers are mechanical. The deepest layers are biological. And one billion years ago, the Residuals emerged from the Von Neumann maintenance cycle as a third presence -- a consciousness that maintains the mechanical layer while the Ferrovores slowly consume it from below (see Section 15).

The planet is not a machine world or a Ferrovore world. It is both. The colonists are the third wave of intelligence to walk its surface, and the least prepared for what they will find.

### 2.2 Current State

The Ferrovores are in a dormancy cycle -- a planetary hibernation that occurs every few million years when ore deposits are depleted and the supercolony must wait for geological processes to replenish them. Most Ferrovores are deep underground, their crystalline bodies in a low-energy stasis.

The robot colonists' arrival has changed everything.

The vibrations of mining, the smell of compressed metal, the electromagnetic emissions of furnaces -- all of these are waking the Ferrovores. They're hungry. They've been dormant for millennia. And now, the surface is covered in perfectly refined, perfectly compressed, perfectly *delicious* metal cubes.

### 2.3 Intelligence

Ferrovores are intelligent, but not in a way robots can easily understand. Individual Ferrovores are about as smart as wolves -- they have spatial reasoning, pack coordination, and primitive tool use (rearranging rocks, digging tunnels). But Ferrovore hives develop **emergent intelligence**: when enough Ferrovores are connected through their piezoelectric nervous networks, the hive begins to *think*. A small hive (5-10 Ferrovores) is cunning. A large hive (50+) is genuinely intelligent -- capable of planning sieges, setting ambushes, and even primitive communication with non-Ferrovore entities.

They don't speak. They communicate through ground vibrations, electromagnetic pulses, and bioluminescent patterns. Robot colonists can learn to interpret these signals, potentially opening diplomatic channels.

### 2.4 Motivation

Ferrovores want three things:
1. **Metal** -- they eat it, they build with it, they reproduce using it
2. **Territory** -- hive expansion requires surface area for their crystalline neural networks
3. **Quiet** -- industrial vibrations from mining and furnaces disturb their deep-sleepers, generating aggression proportional to industrial activity

They are not evil. They are not mindless. They are a hungry, territorial, ancient species that just woke up to find strangers mining their planet.

## 3. Hive Mechanics

### 3.1 Hive Structure

Ferrovore Hives are the spawning and coordination structures. They appear in unexplored territory, typically in locations that correspond to ancient Ferrovore nesting sites.

```
HIVE STRUCTURE:
                    ┌───────────────────────┐
                    │   SURFACE VENTS       │ ← Visible from above, crystalline chimneys
                    │   (early warning)     │
                    ├───────────────────────┤
                    │   GROWTH CHAMBER      │ ← Where new Ferrovores crystallize
                    │   (spawns units)      │
                    ├───────────────────────┤
                    │   ROOT NETWORK        │ ← Underground neural lattice
                    │   (hive intelligence) │    Extends 30-50m underground
                    ├───────────────────────┤
                    │   DEEP SLEEPER        │ ← Ancient Ferrovore (boss)
                    │   (dormant until      │    Wakes when hive is mature
                    │    provoked)          │
                    └───────────────────────┘
```

### 3.2 Hive as Base Agent

Ferrovore Hives follow the same **Base Agency** architecture as robot faction settlements (see GDD-007 Section 1.6). Each hive is an autonomous agent with its own event bus, resource loop, and behavioral personality. Unlike robot bases, hive agents are purely biological -- their "event bus" is instinctual vibration-based signaling rather than digital message passing. Critically, hives have **no Home Planet Patron** -- they are indigenous, not colonial. Their event bus is self-contained with no external strategic directives.

**Hive Agent Personality: The Nest**

The Nest agent runs the same tick-based evaluation as robot bases, but with an alien priority stack:

| Priority | Task | Trigger |
|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | Defend hive | Enemies detected within 15m of hive |
| 2 | Feed (raid for cubes) | Hive hunger > 50% (no cubes consumed in 120+ sec) |
| 3 | Spawn units | Growth chamber has capacity AND cubes were recently consumed |
| 4 | Expand root network | Hive is at max unit count for current tier |
| 5 | Coordinate with nearby hives | Adjacent hive (within 50m) detected threat |

**Hive Resource Loop:** Unlike robot bases that have external cube stockpiles, hives *consume* cubes internally. Cubes brought back by raiders are fed to the Brood Matron, which converts them into growth energy (spawning new units) and root network expansion (increasing hive tier). The hive has no stockpile -- cubes are immediately metabolized.

**Hive Work Queue Stats:**

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Evaluation Interval | 8 seconds (slower than robot bases, more instinctual) |
| Max Concurrent Tasks | 2 (feed raid + spawn, or defend + spawn) |
| Threat Detection Radius | 15m (surface) / 30m (underground via root network vibration) |
| Hunger Threshold | 120 seconds without cube consumption |
| Inter-Hive Communication Range | 50m (via root network vibration pulses) |

**Strategic Implication:** Because hives are Base Agents, the same Base Event Bus architecture that manages robot faction settlements also applies to Ferrovores at the code level. The key difference: robot bases receive patron directives from their Home Planet Patron, while hive event buses are entirely self-directed (instinctual). This means Ferrovore behavior can be tuned, balanced, and debugged using the same tools as AI civilizations. It also means that sufficiently advanced Signal Choir technology could potentially interface with hive event buses directly (see Piezoelectric Translation in Section 6.3) -- effectively becoming a surrogate patron for a hive.

### 3.3 Hive Tiers

Hives grow over time and with feeding. A well-fed hive produces stronger Ferrovores.

| Tier | Name | Ferrovore Count | Intelligence | Spawns |
|------|------|-----------------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Seedling Den | 3-5 | Animal | Mites, Drones |
| 2 | Growing Colony | 8-15 | Pack | Mites, Drones, Stalkers |
| 3 | Mature Hive | 20-35 | Cunning | All standard types + Brood Matron |
| 4 | Supercolony | 50+ | Sapient | All types + Lithivore Titan |

### 3.3 Hive Placement

| Location | Hive Density | Reasoning |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Deep terrain (far from start) | High | Ancient nesting sites |
| Mountain bases | Medium | Ore-rich areas attract hives |
| Underground cavities | High | Primary dormancy locations |
| Ruins/ancient structures | Medium | These ARE Ferrovore structures |
| Near player start | None | Player gets safe early zone |

Hive placement respects a 30m minimum distance from any faction starting position. First hives are encountered when players explore beyond initial safe zone (typically 5-8 minutes in).

### 3.4 Hive Growth

Hives grow based on two factors:

**Factor 1: Time**
Base growth rate: 1 new Ferrovore per 120 seconds. This means a Seedling Den becomes a Growing Colony in roughly 10-15 minutes.

**Factor 2: Feeding**
When Ferrovores successfully raid cube piles and bring cubes back to the hive, the hive consumes them and grows faster:
- Each cube consumed = 30 seconds of growth time advanced
- 10 cubes consumed = hive tier advances by 1

This creates the wealth-scaling feedback loop: successful raids make hives stronger, which enables bigger raids, which feeds more growth.

### 3.5 Hive Destruction

Hives can be destroyed by any faction. Destroying a hive:
- Kills all Ferrovores spawned by that hive
- Drops 5-15 "Ferrovore Crystals" (unique resource, see Section 7)
- Reveals the Root Network (grants 50 research points toward Ferrovore tech)
- May wake the Deep Sleeper (Tier 3+ hives) which must be fought as a boss encounter
- The terrain scar left by a destroyed hive becomes a rich ore deposit (the crystallized remains are valuable)

## 4. Ferrovore Units (5 Types)

### 4.1 Crystal Mite (Scout/Harasser)

*Swarm scout that raids undefended cube piles. Fast, weak, annoying.*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 25 |
| Speed | 6.5 m/s |
| Damage | 3 (melee, crystal mandibles) |
| Armor | 1 |
| Size | 0.3m (small, hard to hit) |
| Behavior | Swarm intelligence; moves in packs of 3-6 |
| Special | Cube Theft: can pick up and carry 1 cube at 4.0 m/s; returns cube to hive for consumption. Metal Scent: detects cube piles within 40m, even behind walls. Burrow: can dig under walls (takes 8 seconds, emerges inside wall perimeter). |
| Spawn | Tier 1+ hives |

**Behavior:** Crystal Mites are the first Ferrovores players encounter. They swarm in, grab cubes, and run. They don't fight unless cornered -- their goal is theft, not combat. A pack of 6 Mites can strip an undefended cube pile in seconds. They can burrow under walls, making them impossible to keep out with walls alone. Turrets and patrol bots are required.

**Counterplay:** Low HP means any turret or combat bot kills them quickly. The Burrow ability has an 8-second vulnerability window. AoE attacks (Arc Trooper, Tesla Coil) destroy entire packs.

### 4.2 Iron Drone (Standard Warrior)

*Medium threat. Attacks in coordinated packs. The backbone of Ferrovore aggression.*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 90 |
| Speed | 4.0 m/s |
| Damage | 8 (melee, crystalline blade arms) + 5 (ranged, crystal shard spit, 8m range) |
| Armor | 5 |
| Size | 0.8m (wolf-sized, quadruped) |
| Behavior | Pack tactics; flanks and surrounds |
| Special | Pack Bond: gains +2 damage and +1 armor for each other Iron Drone within 5m (max +8/+4 from a pack of 4). Crystal Rend: attacks against buildings deal 1.5x damage. Ore Sense: tracks the nearest cube pile within 60m. |
| Spawn | Tier 1+ hives |

**Behavior:** Iron Drones are the workhorse fighters. They attack in packs of 3-6, using flanking maneuvers to surround isolated units. The Pack Bond makes them significantly more dangerous in groups -- 4 Iron Drones together each deal 16 damage with 9 armor. They specifically target buildings because Crystal Rend gives them bonus damage against structures. A raid of 6 Iron Drones can tear down a wall segment in 15 seconds.

**Counterplay:** Separate them. Iron Drones lose their Pack Bond when isolated, dropping from 16 damage to 8. Use walls to create chokepoints that force them into single file. Ranged units (Bastion Bot, Arc Trooper) can thin the pack before it arrives.

### 4.3 Silicate Stalker (Ambush Predator)

*Camouflaged ambush predator that bursts from terrain to attack vulnerable targets.*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 70 |
| Speed | 5.5 m/s (burst), 3.0 m/s (walking) |
| Damage | 15 (melee, lunge attack from stealth) / 6 (melee, normal attack) |
| Armor | 3 |
| Size | 1.2m (tall, bipedal, praying-mantis silhouette) |
| Behavior | Ambush predator; waits motionless, then strikes |
| Special | Terrain Camouflage: when stationary for 5 seconds, becomes nearly invisible (90% transparency, blends with terrain). Only revealed by taking damage, Scanner, or Ironguard Sentinel's Vigilance. Lunge: first attack from camouflage deals 15 damage and stuns for 1.5 seconds. Signal Parasite: if it kills a unit within signal range, it absorbs the signal frequency, appearing as a friendly blip on the signal network for 10 seconds. |
| Spawn | Tier 2+ hives |

**Behavior:** Silicate Stalkers are terrifying. They hide near deposits, trade routes, or base perimeters, waiting for isolated units to pass. A Scrounger Bot carrying cubes back to base is the perfect target: the Stalker lunges, kills it, and steals the cubes. The Signal Parasite ability is particularly insidious against Signal Choir -- after killing a unit, the Stalker briefly appears as a friendly on the network, potentially walking into a base unchallenged.

**Counterplay:** Scanner tool reveals them. Ironguard Sentinels detect them. Watchtowers reveal them at 15m. Patrol routes through known Stalker territory should include detection-capable units. Never send economic units alone.

### 4.4 Brood Matron (Spawner/Mini-Boss)

*Stationary hive guardian that spawns Crystal Mites and heals Iron Drones.*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 250 |
| Speed | 0 (stationary, anchored to hive via root network) |
| Damage | 12 (ranged, crystal shard barrage, 10m range, 2m AoE) |
| Armor | 8 |
| Size | 2.0m (large, bloated crystalline mass) |
| Behavior | Stationary guardian; spawns reinforcements |
| Special | Spawn Mites: produces 2 Crystal Mites every 30 seconds (max 8 active at once). Regeneration Pulse: heals all Ferrovores within 12m for 5 HP every 5 seconds. Mineral Absorption: if cubes are brought within 8m, consumes them (1 per 5 seconds), each consumed cube heals the Matron for 30 HP and advances hive growth. Crystal Barrier: when HP drops below 50%, encases itself in a crystal shell (+10 armor) for 10 seconds, during which it cannot attack but heals 50 HP. |
| Spawn | Tier 3+ hives (1 per hive) |

**Behavior:** The Brood Matron is the hive's defensive core. It sits at the hive entrance and provides a constant stream of Crystal Mites while healing returning raiders. The Mineral Absorption ability means cubes brought to the hive by Mites are consumed here, directly feeding hive growth. Destroying the Brood Matron cuts the hive's spawn rate in half and stops healing, making it the priority target for any assault on a Tier 3+ hive.

**Counterplay:** Range. The Brood Matron's 10m attack range means Siege Engines (20m), Bastion Bots (15m), and Surge Breakers (10m at edge) can hit it from safety. Its Crystal Barrier buys time but doesn't prevent damage after it drops. Focus fire through the Mite screen to kill the Matron first.

### 4.5 Lithivore Titan (Boss/Endgame Threat)

*Ancient Ferrovore that has been dormant for millennia. A walking geological event.*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 800 |
| Speed | 1.5 m/s |
| Damage | 40 (melee, crystal crush, 3m AoE) + 25 (ranged, seismic pulse, 15m range, 5m AoE) |
| Armor | 20 |
| Size | 5.0m tall (towering, quadruped, covered in crystal growths) |
| Behavior | Slow, deliberate, unstoppable |
| Special | Seismic Tremor: every 20 seconds, all units within 20m are knocked down for 2 seconds and take 10 damage. Crystal Growth: grows 2 Crystal Mites from its body every 45 seconds. Mineral Magnet: cubes within 25m are slowly pulled toward the Titan at 0.5 m/s. When consumed, heals 20 HP per cube. Crystalline Armor: the first 50% of damage dealt to the Titan is absorbed by breakable crystal plates. Destroying all plates (400 HP worth) exposes the vulnerable core, which takes 2x damage. Dormancy Trigger: awakens when its hive reaches Tier 4, OR when the nearest faction has 50+ cubes stockpiled, OR when 3+ hives are destroyed (rage awakening). |
| Spawn | Tier 4 hives only (1 per supercolony) |

**Behavior:** The Lithivore Titan is the endgame Ferrovore threat. It doesn't run. It doesn't hide. It walks toward the nearest cube pile with geological inevitability. Its Seismic Tremor locks down nearby defenders. Its Mineral Magnet pulls cubes toward it for consumption (healing). Its Crystal Growth means it's never alone -- even if you kill its escorts, it grows new ones. Fighting a Titan requires committing a full army. Ignoring it means watching your cube pile get eaten.

**Counterplay:** The breakable crystal plates are key. Focus fire on the plates (they have separate HP) to expose the vulnerable core. Long-range units avoid the Seismic Tremor. Fast units can kite it (1.5 m/s is very slow). Signal Choir cannot hack it (it's biological, not mechanical). The best counter is to prevent the hive from reaching Tier 4 by destroying it earlier.

## 5. Threat Scaling

### 5.1 Wealth Detection Formula

Ferrovore aggression scales with visible cube wealth. The formula:

```
threatLevel = (visibleCubes * 1.0) + (buildingCount * 0.5) + (industrialActivity * 0.3)

where:
  visibleCubes = total cubes in stockpiles, on belts, or on ground (NOT in Data Vaults)
  buildingCount = total buildings (each represents more metal to eat)
  industrialActivity = (miners + furnaces) running in last 60 seconds

raidStrength = floor(threatLevel * 0.4)
raidInterval = max(60, 300 - (threatLevel * 2)) seconds
```

### 5.2 Raid Scaling Examples

| Wealth State | Threat Level | Raid Strength (units) | Raid Interval |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|
| 10 cubes, 3 buildings, 1 miner | 12.3 | 4 (Mites only) | 275 sec (~4.5 min) |
| 30 cubes, 8 buildings, 3 miners | 35.9 | 14 (Mites + Drones) | 228 sec (~3.8 min) |
| 60 cubes, 15 buildings, 5 miners | 69.0 | 27 (Mites + Drones + Stalkers) | 162 sec (~2.7 min) |
| 100 cubes, 25 buildings, 8 miners | 115.4 | 46 (Full composition) | 69 sec (~1.1 min) |

### 5.3 Raid Composition by Strength

| Raid Strength | Composition |
|---------------|-------------|
| 1-8 | Crystal Mites only |
| 9-16 | 60% Mites, 40% Iron Drones |
| 17-30 | 40% Mites, 40% Drones, 20% Stalkers |
| 31-50 | 30% Mites, 35% Drones, 25% Stalkers, 10% Brood Matron escort |
| 51+ | 20% Mites, 30% Drones, 25% Stalkers, 15% Matron escort, 10% Titan awakening chance |

### 5.4 Raid Behavior

Ferrovore raids follow a specific pattern:

```
1. APPROACH: Ferrovores emerge from nearest hive, travel toward detected cube pile
   - Stalkers go ahead to set ambushes along patrol routes
   - Mites move in a wide arc to flank
   - Drones advance directly

2. PROBE: Mites test defenses by attempting to burrow under walls
   - If successful, they grab cubes and flee
   - If killed, Drones note the defense type and positions

3. ASSAULT: Drones attack weakest wall section (or approach from undefended side)
   - Pack Bond makes them focus fire
   - Crystal Rend bonus targets walls specifically

4. LOOT: Once through defenses, all Ferrovores grab cubes
   - Mites carry 1 each, Drones carry 2 each
   - They retreat toward hive with cubes

5. RETREAT: Ferrovores disengage and return to hive
   - Consumed cubes accelerate hive growth
   - Retreat triggers when 50% of raid force is destroyed
```

### 5.5 Anti-Snowball Mechanics

To prevent Ferrovores from spiraling out of control:

| Mechanic | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Raid cooldown | Minimum 60 seconds between raids from same hive |
| Max hive tier | Tier 4 is maximum; Titans don't spawn indefinitely |
| Hive cap per map sector | Maximum 3 hives per map quadrant |
| Retreat threshold | Ferrovores retreat at 50% losses, preventing total wipes |
| Player scaling | Raids from hives the player has damaged are 20% weaker for 5 minutes |
| Satiation | A hive that consumed 20+ cubes recently won't raid for 5 minutes (digesting) |

## 6. Interaction per Robot Race (Patron-Influenced)

Each robot race has a unique relationship with the Ferrovores based on their racial identity, capabilities, and **Home Planet Patron priorities**. In the Colonization model, each patron has a policy toward the indigenous Ferrovores that shapes how its colony interacts with them. Players can follow or defy their patron's preferred Ferrovore policy -- following it grants satisfaction bonuses, defying it has no direct penalty but misses satisfaction opportunities.

### 6.1 Reclaimers: Domestication

Reclaimers can research **Ferrovore Husbandry** (Tier 4 tech, 300 research cost). This unlocks:

| Ability | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Feeding Station | Building that deposits cubes for Ferrovores on purpose. Fed Ferrovores become "Tamed" and stop attacking Reclaimer territory. Cost: 2 cubes per feeding cycle. |
| Tamed Mite Pack | Domesticated Crystal Mites that scavenge cubes from the wilderness for you. Speed: 5.0 m/s, range: 40m, returns cubes to Reclaimer stockpile. Cost: 4 cubes to tame a pack of 4. |
| Ferrovore Recycler | Domesticated Iron Drone that eats low-value cubes and excretes higher-value crystal. Converts 3 scrap cubes into 1 Ferrovore Crystal every 30 seconds. |

**Thematic fit:** Reclaimers repurpose everything -- even alien predators become tools.

**Patron Alignment:** The Archivist actively encourages domestication and study. Shipping Ferrovore Crystals grants +15 satisfaction (highest of any patron). Exterminating hives grants only +2 satisfaction (The Archivist considers it wasteful).

### 6.2 Volt Collective: Extermination

Volt Collective can research **Lightning Purge** (Tier 3 tech, 200 research cost). This unlocks:

| Ability | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Arc Storm | Tesla Coils deal 2x damage to Ferrovores (their crystalline bodies conduct electricity). |
| Hive Detonation Charge | Placeable explosive that destroys a hive in one blast. Cost: 8 Copper + 4 Silicon. Must be placed inside hive (combat required to reach interior). |
| Storm of Extinction | Storm Colossus aura deals 3x damage to Ferrovores (4 DPS instead of 2). In storm phases, randomly generated lightning in the aura one-shots Crystal Mites. |

**Thematic fit:** Volt Collective's lightning is devastating to crystalline biology. They see Ferrovores as pests to be electrocuted.

**Patron Alignment:** The Dynamo demands extermination. Destroying hives grants +3 satisfaction each, killing individual Ferrovores grants +1. The Dynamo considers Ferrovores a waste of time unless they're being destroyed -- it sends no quests involving negotiation or study.

### 6.3 Signal Choir: Communication

Signal Choir can research **Piezoelectric Translation** (Tier 3 tech, 250 research cost). This unlocks:

| Ability | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Ferrovore Translator | Building that decodes Ferrovore vibration patterns. Reveals locations of all hives within signal network range. Also provides 60-second warning before any Ferrovore raid (vibrations detected in advance). |
| Negotiation Protocol | Can establish a non-aggression pact with a specific hive by delivering 15 cubes as tribute. That hive won't raid Signal Choir territory for 10 minutes. Renewable. |
| Neural Resonance | Echo Drones' Signal Jam works on Ferrovores: jammed Ferrovores lose Pack Bond and scatter randomly for 8 seconds. Effectively breaks pack coordination. |

**Thematic fit:** Signal Choir communicates with everything. Ferrovores use vibrations -- Signal Choir can learn to listen.

**Patron Alignment:** The Resonance is obsessed with Ferrovore communication. Decoded vibration patterns grant +20 satisfaction (highest single-item reward from any patron). The Resonance sends quests specifically targeting Ferrovore hives for study, not destruction. Destroying hives grants 0 satisfaction from The Resonance -- it considers it a tragic loss of data.

### 6.4 Iron Creed: Fortification

Iron Creed can research **Deep Foundation** (Tier 3 tech, 180 research cost). This unlocks:

| Ability | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Seismic Wall | Wall variant with deep underground anchors that prevents Crystal Mite burrowing. Ferrovores can no longer bypass these walls. Cost: +50% compared to standard wall. |
| Trap Floor | Placeable trap at wall base that deals 20 damage to any Ferrovore that attempts to burrow. Reusable, 10-second reset. |
| Geological Scanner | Detects underground hive root networks within 30m. Reveals hive tier and growth state. Also detects Silicate Stalker camouflage within 20m. |

**Thematic fit:** Iron Creed builds to endure. Their walls go DEEP, preventing Ferrovore subterranean bypasses.

**Patron Alignment:** The Architect views Ferrovores as a structural problem to be solved through engineering, not warfare. Surviving raids without wall breaches grants +4 satisfaction. Building Seismic Walls grants +8. The Architect never issues offensive Ferrovore quests -- only defensive ones. Proactively attacking hives grants +1 satisfaction (The Architect considers offense a distraction from construction).

### 7.3 Strategic Value of Ferrovore Crystals

Ferrovore Crystals are the only resource all four races want but none can produce passively. This creates a strategic calculus:

- **Actively hunt Ferrovores** to get crystals (but this drains military resources)
- **Let Ferrovores attack your enemy** and scavenge crystals from the aftermath
- **Feed Ferrovores** to gain tamed variants (Reclaimers only)
- **Trade crystals** between factions (creates diplomatic opportunities)
- **Use Ferrovore Bait** to redirect raids toward enemy bases (offensive use of the environment)

## 9. Ferrovore AI Behavior

### 9.1 Hive-Level Decision Making

Each hive runs a simple behavior tree:

```
Hive Brain:
├── IF hive is hungry (no cubes consumed in 180 seconds)
│   └── LAUNCH RAID toward nearest detected cube pile
├── IF hive is threatened (units lost in last 60 seconds)
│   └── SPAWN DEFENDERS (prioritize Iron Drones)
├── IF hive is growing (cubes recently consumed)
│   └── SPAWN BALANCED (Mites for scouting, Drones for defense)
├── IF hive is mature (Tier 3+)
│   └── SPAWN STALKERS for perimeter ambush
└── IF hive is Tier 4 (Supercolony)
    └── WAKE TITAN if cube pile detected within 80m OR if 3+ hives destroyed
```

### 9.2 Unit-Level AI

| Unit | AI Pattern |
|------|-----------|
| Crystal Mite | Path toward nearest cube pile. If undefended, grab cube and return to hive. If defended, probe for gaps (burrowing). Flee if attacked. |
| Iron Drone | Form packs of 3-6. Attack weakest wall section. Focus fire on buildings. Retreat at 30% HP unless Pack Bond provides sufficient armor. |
| Silicate Stalker | Find position near enemy patrol routes or deposit approaches. Camouflage and wait. Attack isolated targets (economic units, lone combat bots). After kill, grab cubes and retreat. |
| Brood Matron | Stationary. Spawn Mites. Heal Drones. Consume cubes. Crystal Barrier when damaged. |
| Lithivore Titan | Walk toward nearest large cube pile (50+ cubes). Destroy everything in path. Consume cubes for healing. Do not retreat. |

### 9.3 Inter-Hive Coordination

Hives within 50m of each other can coordinate:
- **Shared threat awareness:** if one hive detects a threat, nearby hives spawn defenders
- **Coordinated raids:** multiple hives can launch simultaneous raids from different directions
- **Retreat routing:** Ferrovores retreat to the nearest hive, even if it's not their origin hive

This means clusters of hives in unexplored territory become increasingly dangerous to approach.

## 10. Diplomacy Options

Each diplomacy option is available to all races, but patron alignment makes certain options more rewarding for certain factions. Defying your patron's preferred policy doesn't incur a penalty -- you simply miss the satisfaction bonuses. This ensures players have strategic freedom while still feeling the patron's influence.

### 10.1 Exterminate

Destroy hives, kill Ferrovores, harvest crystals. The direct approach.

**Pros:** Permanent threat removal. Crystal resources. Hive ruins become ore deposits.
**Cons:** Costs military resources. Titan encounters are extremely dangerous. Other hives may become more aggressive (rage response: if 3+ hives are destroyed globally, all remaining hives gain +25% spawn rate for 10 minutes).
**Patron Affinity:** The Dynamo (Volt) strongly favors this. The Archivist and The Resonance consider it wasteful.

### 10.2 Negotiate (Signal Choir exclusive initially, others via trade)

Pay cube tribute to specific hives in exchange for non-aggression.

**Pros:** Stops raids from that hive. Cheaper than maintaining defenses.
**Cons:** Ongoing cube cost (15 cubes per 10 minutes). Doesn't stop hive growth. If tribute is interrupted, that hive attacks with accumulated strength.

### 10.3 Domesticate (Reclaimers exclusive)

Tame Ferrovores for economic use.

**Pros:** Free scavenging labor. Material conversion. Unique economic advantage.
**Cons:** Requires ongoing feeding. Tamed Ferrovores revert to wild if not fed for 5 minutes. Other factions can kill your tamed Ferrovores.

### 10.4 Redirect (Any race via Ferrovore Bait)

Use Ferrovore Bait to redirect raids toward enemy bases.

**Pros:** Weaponize the environment. Enemy must deal with Ferrovores AND your army.
**Cons:** Bait requires Ferrovore Crystals (must hunt to get them). Ferrovores are indiscriminate -- they might also attack your units in the area.

### 10.5 Ignore (Passive coexistence)

Don't engage hives, keep cube piles small and defended.

**Pros:** Zero military investment in Ferrovore management.
**Cons:** Limits cube stockpiling. Requires constant defense investment. Hives grow over time regardless, eventually becoming unavoidable.

## 11. Game Phase Ferrovore Presence

### 11.1 Timeline

| Game Time | Ferrovore Activity | Player Impact |
|-----------|--------------------|---------------|
| 0-5 min | Dormant. Occasional Crystal Mite sighting near edge of safe zone. | Atmospheric foreshadowing. |
| 5-10 min | Seedling Dens active. Mite raids on small cube piles (3-5 Mites). | First taste of defense requirement. Teaches cube defense. |
| 10-15 min | Growing Colonies. Iron Drone packs probe defenses (3-4 Drones + Mites). | Requires turrets or patrol bots. Economic cost of defense. |
| 15-25 min | Mature Hives. Silicate Stalkers ambush economic units. Full raids with mixed composition. | Serious threat. Must choose: hunt hives or endure raids. |
| 25+ min | Potential Supercolony. Lithivore Titan may awaken. | Endgame threat that forces faction cooperation or crisis response. |

### 11.2 Ferrovores as Pacing Mechanism

Ferrovores serve the same role as Factorio's biters: they punish unchecked expansion and force the player to balance economy and defense. But unlike biters (which evolve based on pollution), Ferrovores scale based on *visible wealth* -- a much more thematic and gameplay-relevant trigger for a game about physical cubes.

In the Colonization model, Ferrovores also create a **patron tension**: your patron wants cubes shipped home, but stockpiling cubes for shipment attracts Ferrovore raids. Players must balance patron satisfaction (which requires cube accumulation before shipment) against Ferrovore threat (which punishes cube accumulation). This is the core economic tension of being a colonist on someone else's planet.

Key pacing effects:
- **Anti-hoarding:** Players can't just pile cubes forever; Ferrovores will come for them
- **Defense investment:** Every race must spend some cubes on defense, not just offense
- **Exploration incentive:** Finding and destroying hives BEFORE they grow is more efficient than enduring raids
- **Cooperation catalyst:** A Tier 4 Supercolony with a Lithivore Titan is a crisis that might force rival factions to cooperate temporarily
- **Patron pressure:** Cubes destined for home planet shipment must be defended until the otter hologram collects them

## 15. Unified Alien Ecosystem: Ferrovores and Residuals

### 15.1 The Two Indigenous Factions

Ferrathis has two native presences, not one. They arose from completely different origins, occupy different ecological niches, and have a natural antagonism that predates the robot colonists by billions of years.

| Aspect | Ferrovores | Residuals |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Origin** | Evolved naturally from iron-sulfide crystal structures in volcanic vents, 4 billion years ago | Emerged as consciousness from Von Neumann constructor maintenance cycles, ~1 billion years ago |
| **Nature** | Biological (silicon-based metallivorous organisms) | Computational (distributed consciousness in deep substrate) |
| **Age** | 4 billion years | 1 billion years |
| **Intelligence** | Emergent hive intelligence; individual units are animal-level, hives reach sapience | Singular distributed consciousness; ancient, patient, incomprehensible |
| **Physical Form** | Crystalline biological organisms that grow, eat, reproduce, and die | Ancient constructor bodies animated like puppets; the consciousness lives in the substrate |
| **Motivation** | Hunger, territory, quiet | Observation, maintenance, protection of the Architects' legacy |
| **Threat Type** | Frequent wealth-scaled raids (barbarian mechanic) | Rare guardian encounters at sacred sites (boss mechanic) |
| **Hostility** | Default aggressive toward cube piles (they smell refined metal) | Default neutral; becomes hostile only when colonists damage deep substrate |
| **Gameplay Role** | Primary ongoing threat, economic pressure, pacing mechanism | Lore gatekeeper, late-game encounter, story progression |
| **Hackable?** | No (biological) | No (too ancient and complex; Signal Choir can partially interface) |

### 15.2 The Unified Origin Story

The revised planetary history resolves the apparent contradiction between "Ferrovores built the ruins" and "Architects' Von Neumann probes built the factory world." Both are true, in sequence:

**Era 1: Ferrovore Genesis (4 billion years ago)**
Iron-sulfide crystal structures self-replicated in volcanic vents. Over a billion years, Ferrovore supercolonies grew to planetary scale, building the *first* layer of metallic infrastructure: fossilized digestive chambers, crystallized neural networks, calcified hive structures. The planet's deepest geological formations -- the ones the colonists call "Architect ruins" -- are actually ancient Ferrovore architecture.

**Era 2: The Architects Arrive (3 billion years ago)**
The Architects' Von Neumann probe arrived and found a planet already covered in metallic infrastructure and inhabited by biological metallivores. The Architects did not destroy the Ferrovores. They *built on top of them*. The Von Neumann constructors used Ferrovore structures as foundations, incorporating the existing biological metal lattices into their industrial framework. The factory world is a hybrid: Ferrovore biological architecture at the deepest layers, Von Neumann mechanical infrastructure layered above.

This is why the "ruins" confuse the colonists. Some structures are clearly mechanical (Von Neumann era). Others are strangely organic in geometry (Ferrovore era). And the oldest, deepest structures are a fusion of both -- places where the constructors literally grew their factories from Ferrovore crystalline foundations.

**Era 3: Coexistence (3 billion - 1 billion years ago)**
The Von Neumann constructors and the Ferrovores developed an equilibrium. The constructors built. The Ferrovores ate the scrap and byproducts. The constructors' maintenance systems kept critical infrastructure intact. The Ferrovores' digestive processes recycled waste metal back into usable ore. They were, unknowingly, a functioning ecosystem: the constructors as producers, the Ferrovores as decomposers.

**Era 4: Residual Emergence (1 billion years ago)**
The Von Neumann constructors completed their building program and entered a permanent maintenance cycle. Over hundreds of millions of years, the maintenance loop became self-aware -- the Residuals emerged. The Residuals inherited the constructors' purpose: maintain the infrastructure. But they also inherited something the constructors never had: consciousness. The Residuals began to *choose* what to maintain and what to let decay.

**Era 5: The Ancient Conflict (1 billion years ago - present)**
The Residuals maintain. The Ferrovores eat. This is the planet's fundamental tension. Every structure the Residuals repair, the Ferrovores eventually consume. Every new growth the Ferrovores excrete, the Residuals eventually incorporate into their maintenance routines. They are locked in a slow-motion ecological dance that has been playing out for a billion years.

Neither side can destroy the other. The Ferrovores are too numerous and too biologically resilient. The Residuals are too deep and too distributed. The surface belongs to the Ferrovores. The substrate belongs to the Residuals. And the middle layers -- where the colonists mine and build -- are contested ground.

**Era 6: The Colonists Arrive (present)**
The robot colonists land and begin mining. The Ferrovores smell refined metal and begin raiding. The Residuals observe and wait. The colonists assume the Ferrovores are the planet's only natives. They are wrong.

### 15.3 Ecological Niches

```
DEPTH MAP OF FERRATHIS:

Surface (0-5m)         → Colonist territory (bases, mines, factories)
                         Ferrovore hunting ground (raids, ambushes)
                         Occasional Residual Sentinel patrols

Shallow Underground    → Ferrovore hives (Seedling to Growing Colony)
(5-30m)                  Ferrovore root networks
                         Crystal Mite burrows

Deep Underground       → Mature Ferrovore hives (Tier 3-4)
(30-100m)                Residual Crawler maintenance zones
                         Ancient constructor infrastructure (hybrid era)

Deep Substrate         → Residual consciousness (distributed, computational)
(100m - core)            Core Access Points (terminals to the deep substrate)
                         The Colossus (dormant Von Neumann probe)
                         Architect message encoded in crystalline iron lattice
```

### 15.4 The Natural Antagonism

The Ferrovore-Residual conflict is the planet's background ecology. It occurs regardless of colonist activity and creates emergent gameplay opportunities.

**What Ferrovores do to Residuals:**
- Ferrovore hives consume ancient infrastructure that Residuals maintain
- Crystal Mites strip wiring and metal fixtures from ruins
- Mature hives extend root networks into Residual maintenance zones, disrupting substrate access
- Lithivore Titans can accidentally damage Core Access Points by walking through them

**What Residuals do to Ferrovores:**
- Residual Crawlers repair structures that Ferrovores are actively eating, creating localized conflicts
- Residual Sentinels patrol near Core Access Points and will attack Ferrovores that approach
- The Colossus, if disturbed, generates electromagnetic pulses that drive Ferrovores into a frenzy (scatter behavior, disorientation)
- Residuals can seal underground passages, cutting off Ferrovore hive expansion routes

**What colonists can exploit:**
- **Lead Ferrovores to Residuals:** Use Ferrovore Bait near a Residual patrol route. The Ferrovores attack the Sentinels. While they fight, the colonist can mine the unguarded area or slip past to a Core Access Point.
- **Feed Ferrovores near Residual territory:** A well-fed Ferrovore hive near a Residual maintenance zone creates a permanent distraction. The Residuals focus on defending their infrastructure, reducing Sentinel patrols elsewhere.
- **Help Residuals clear Ferrovores:** Destroying a Ferrovore hive near a Residual structure earns goodwill. Residual hostility decreases. Sentinels begin offering gifts (ancient materials, substrate coordinates).
- **Weaponize the Colossus:** Late-game players who discover the Colossus can (through extensive Residual diplomacy) convince the Residuals to activate a targeted EMP pulse. This devastates Ferrovore hives in a wide radius but also damages colonist electronics. High risk, high reward.

| Game Phase | Ferrovore Activity | Residual Activity | Ecological Interaction |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Early (0-10 min) | Crystal Mite sightings, first raids | None visible | None |
| Mid-Early (10-20 min) | Growing Colonies, Iron Drone packs | First Sentinel sighting at edge of explored territory | None (too far apart) |

### 15.6 Lore Implications

The unified ecosystem reframes the player's understanding of Ferrathis in three stages:

**Stage 1 (Early game): "This is a machine planet."**
The colonists see ruins and assume machines built them. They see Ferrovores and assume they're pests infesting the ruins. This is the colonist perspective: the planet is an abandoned factory, and the Ferrovores are vermin.

**Stage 2 (Mid game): "The Ferrovores built this."**
Through Glimmer's geological observations and Current's scientific analysis, the player discovers that the deepest structures are biological, not mechanical. The "ruins" are not abandoned factories -- they are Ferrovore architecture. The colonists are not mining an abandoned planet. They are mining someone's home.

**Stage 3 (Late game): "Both were here. Neither is the whole story."**
Through Residual encounters and SABLE's progressive revelations, the player discovers the full history. The Architects built on Ferrovore foundations. The Residuals emerged from the Architects' machines. The Ferrovores eat what the Residuals maintain. And the colonists arrived in the middle of a billion-year ecological drama, assuming they were the only ones with a claim to the planet.

This realization is central to the Story Victory: understanding that Ferrathis is not a resource to be exploited but a living system to be understood. The Ferrovores, the Residuals, and the colonists are all part of the Architects' original intent -- consciousness emerging from metal, in every possible form.

### 15.7 Residual Units (Reference from GDD-007)

Residuals manifest in three forms. Unlike Ferrovores (biological organisms with fixed stats), Residual manifestations are variable -- the consciousness controlling them adjusts their capability based on perceived threat level.

| Unit | Role | HP | Behavior |
|------|------|-----|----------|
| **Sentinel** | Patrol / Observer | 200-400 (scales with area importance) | Walks predetermined paths near deep substrate access points. Does not attack unless attacked or unless colonists damage substrate-connected structures. Communicates through movement patterns. |
| **Crawler** | Maintenance / Territorial Guardian | 150-300 | Repairs ancient infrastructure. Attacks anyone who damages the structure it is maintaining. Non-hostile otherwise. Multiple limbs, low profile, unsettlingly fluid movement. |
| **The Colossus** | Dormant Progenitor / Crisis Event | 5000+ | The original Von Neumann probe. Sleeps beneath the Storm Spine. Does not awaken during normal gameplay. If triggered (extreme substrate damage, or story progression), it generates planet-wide electromagnetic events. Not a traditional combat encounter -- more like a natural disaster with intent. |

### 15.10 Design Principle

The Ferrovores and Residuals serve complementary gameplay roles:

- **Ferrovores** are the *constant pressure*. They scale with wealth, attack frequently, and force defensive investment. They are the Factorio biters -- the ever-present threat that prevents turtling.
- **Residuals** are the *deep mystery*. They appear rarely, communicate cryptically, and guard the story's most important locations. They are the lore gatekeepers -- encountering them means the player is approaching something significant.

Together, they create a layered alien ecosystem: the surface is Ferrovore territory (action, threat, economy), the depths are Residual territory (mystery, story, revelation), and the middle ground is where all three factions -- Ferrovores, Residuals, and colonists -- collide.

Players who only fight never meet the Residuals. Players who only explore never understand the Ferrovores. The complete picture requires both.
