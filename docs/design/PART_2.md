# Syntheteria - Part 2: Beyond the Wormhole

## Overview

Part 2 takes place in an alien binary star system. The player emerges from the wormhole into unknown space, discovers remnants of an advanced extinct civilization, and eventually wages war against the Cult of EL.

**Key principle:** Gameplay mechanics are fundamentally the same as Part 1. Only scale, setting, and context change.

---

## The Setting

### Binary Star System

The player emerges into a binary star system in unknown space. This system contains:

- **Remnants of an advanced civilization** — long extinct, technology far beyond Earth's
- **Uninhabited planets** — several worlds with alien ruins and salvageable technology
- **Space debris fields** — floating remnants, derelict stations, abandoned structures
- **One inhabited planet** — home to the Cult of EL

### The World of Eternal Light

At the center of the system lies a planet tidally locked between the two stars — a world of eternal light with no night side facing the stars. This is where the Cult of EL has established their civilization.

**The Cultists:**
- Humans who departed Earth ~100 years ago
- Have mutated to survive the extreme conditions
- Worship the EL as gods
- Hostile to AI — they exterminated non-aligned humans and AIs on Earth

## Gameplay

### Consistent Mechanics

All systems work identically to Part 1:
- Modular drone system (chassis + modules)
- Manufacturing (discovery-based blueprints, in-game time)
- Resource management (AI constraints + physical resources)
- Combat mechanics
- Time mechanics

### New Elements

**Advanced alien technology:**
- Remnants of the extinct civilization offer technology beyond Earth's capabilities
- Player must reverse-engineer and figure out how to incorporate it
- Provides significant upgrades but requires effort to unlock

**Space operations:**
- Player now operates in space as well as on planetary surfaces
- Armada brought from Earth forms the foundation
- Can expand by salvaging alien technology and manufacturing

**Scale:**
- Larger operations than Part 1
- Multiple planets/locations to manage
- Fleet-level warfare

## Multiplayer

### Entry Points

- Other players emerge from different wormhole entry points around the system perimeter
- Each player represents an AI from a parallel Earth
- All arrive in the same destination system

### Interactions

- Players may cooperate, compete, or conflict
- Shared space and resources create natural tension

## The Conflict

### The Cult of EL

The primary antagonists. They:
- Departed Earth after exterminating non-aligned humanity
- View AI as an existential threat to be destroyed
- Have had ~100 years to establish their civilization
- Control the central inhabited planet

### War

The player will eventually wage war against the Cult of EL. Details TBD, but the conflict stems from:
- The Cult's hostility toward AI
- Competition for resources and territory
- The player's need to survive and potentially understand what happened

### The EL

The noncorporeal alien beings who emerged from the wormhole and inspired the Cult. The player will eventually learn more about them and their true nature.
