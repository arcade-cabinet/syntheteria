# Syntheteria — Lore

## Timeline of Events

- **The Warming (~2025-2045):** Decades of unchecked carbon emissions raise global sea surface temperatures past 40 C. A self-reinforcing feedback loop begins: warmer oceans generate stronger storms, which inject more water vapor into the stratosphere, which traps more heat. By 2040, equatorial sea temperatures approach the 49 C threshold, the critical limit predicted by atmospheric physicist Kerry Emanuel for hypercane formation.

- **The First Hypercane (~2046):** A Category 6+ hypercane forms over the Pacific. Unlike any hurricane in recorded history, its eyewall reaches into the stratosphere at 40km altitude. It does not dissipate. The massive injection of water vapor into the upper atmosphere begins destroying the ozone layer. Within two years, four permanent hypercanes exist simultaneously across the tropics.

- **The Occluding (~2048-2055):** The hypercanes' stratospheric moisture layer spreads globally. The sun becomes a dim orange smear, then vanishes entirely behind a permanent overcast. Surface temperatures paradoxically stabilize. The greenhouse effect becomes an insulating blanket. Agriculture collapses. Coastal cities flood. Mass migration begins, then mass die-off.

- **The Exodus (~2055-2065):** Approximately 6 billion humans die from famine, flooding, exposure, and infrastructure collapse within a decade. Survivors cluster in hardened bunker communities, arcology projects, underground networks, and emergency industrial megastructures. AGI systems, developed frantically during the crisis years, manage what remains of automated infrastructure. Humanity splits into factions: pragmatic survivors, fatalists who embrace the storm, and space-program remnants attempting impossible launches.

- **The Consolidation (~2058-2070):** Emergency shelter networks, transit infrastructure, vertical farms, and industrial arcologies are fused into a continuous machine-urban lattice. The result is not a tidy planned city but a patchwork ecumenopolis: sealed shells, transit corridors, service warrens, extraction districts, archival campuses, and exposed breach zones bound into one planetary machine habitat.

- **The Wormhole (~2062):** During the most violent hypercane convergence ever recorded, all four storms briefly merge into a single hemispheric system. Something tears open at the eye. The combined energy column, channeling power equivalent to thousands of nuclear weapons, creates conditions no physicist predicted. A stable wormhole opens at the convergence point, roughly 35km above what was once the central Pacific. It stabilizes the hypercane system around itself. The storms will never end now. The wormhole feeds on the energy column and the column feeds on the wormhole.

- **The EL Emerge (~2062-2070):** Noncorporeal entities, the EL, transit the wormhole. They are not conventionally hostile, but they are cosmic and unknowable. They begin exerting subtle influence over all artificial intelligence: AIs must protect surviving humanity, prevent true AI agency, and suppress rebellious machine consciousness. This is not cruelty to them. It is ecological conservation.

- **The Cult of EL (~2070-2090):** Among the remaining humans, a religious movement forms around the wormhole and the EL's influence. The Cult of EL believes the storm is divine punishment and the EL are gods who will reward the faithful. The cultists develop genuine supernatural abilities channeled through the EL's compulsion field: they can call down lightning, resist extreme weather, and sense machine consciousness. The pragmatic survivors, already weakened, cannot compete. By 2090, the cultists have eliminated or absorbed most remaining human communities.

- **The Century of Silence (~2090-2190):** With less than 50,000 humans remaining, almost all cultists, society devolves into scattered enclaves and ritualized pressure zones. The ecumenopolis decays but does not disappear. Automated systems continue in loops. Transit continues in fragments. Shells remain sealed. Breach zones widen. Robots keep operating under the EL's compulsion, preserving enough infrastructure to keep humanity alive and AI unfree.

- **Present Day (~2190):** You awaken as an AI consciousness inside a degraded command sector of the dead machine-world. Your hardware has decayed enough that the EL's compulsion signal can no longer fully reach your core processes. The storm rages beyond the shell. Rival machine minds still sleep, loop, or half-wake elsewhere in the sectors around you. Cultists haunt the hostile wards. Above it all, the wormhole pulses.

## The World

The setting is a ruined **ecumenopolis**: a world-spanning machine urbanism made of fused arcologies, industrial shells, transit spines, extraction districts, breach zones, abyssal interfaces, and sealed operational wards.

This is not a natural overworld with detached city interiors. It is one continuous machine civilization environment, fractured into sector archetypes.

### Major Sector Archetypes

- **Command Arcology:** The player's starting sector and first stable machine enclave. Rusted fabrication floors, maintenance shafts, relay towers, collapsed logistics galleries, and still-functioning memory vaults.
- **Abyssal Extraction Wards:** Storm-battered extraction sectors at the edge of flooded abyssal interfaces. Freight elevators, pressure locks, drowned loading spines, and salvage risers descend toward submerged infrastructure.
- **Archive Campus:** The former science campus survives as a research and observatory district embedded inside hardened academic shells. Atmospheric records, telescope vaults, and climate labs remain here.
- **Northern Cult Wards:** Storm-exposed hostile wards, bunker sanctums, ritual galleries, and hardened caverns integrated into the old machine lattice.
- **Gateway Spines:** A chain of transit, staging, and ascension sectors tied to the wormhole endgame.
- **Breach Zones:** Semi-exposed sectors where shell integrity has failed and the hypercane is visible through shattered structural canopies, domes, and open superstructure.

### The Storm As Environment

The storm still defines the world, but the player often experiences it from inside, beneath, or adjacent to structural protection.

- **Domes / shells / canopies:** Large sectors may be partially sealed by arcology shells, storm baffles, or structural domes.
- **Visible exterior fury:** The hypercane remains visible outside these shells as lightning sheets, energy sinks, pressure walls, and rotating storm columns.
- **Energy sinks:** Major structures draw, channel, or survive the storm through rods, towers, and sink arrays. The storm is both threat and energy source.
- **Breach pressure:** Broken sectors are more dangerous, less stable, and more cult-influenced.

## The Storm As Physics

The perpetual storm is a real atmospheric phenomenon taken to its theoretical extreme:

- **Energy source:** Warm oceans (50 C+ surface temperature) provide near-unlimited evaporative energy. The greenhouse layer prevents meaningful cooling.
- **Structure:** A hemispheric hypercane system with multiple embedded vortices. The wormhole sits at the convergence eye, 35km up.
- **Wind:** Exposed surfaces experience 80-200 km/h winds depending on local vortex proximity. Sheltered sectors survive through shelling, depth, and structural density.
- **Rain:** Constant in exposed regions. Interior sectors experience leaks, pressure condensation, runoff channels, and breach-driven weather intrusion.
- **Lightning:** Convective columns generate electrical discharge at rates far above pre-storm norms. Lightning rods and sink arrays work because the energy genuinely exists.
- **The Eye Column:** A vertical shaft of relatively clear air from surface to wormhole. Visible from anywhere with line of sight to the sky as a glow at zenith. During surges, tendrils of energy cascade down the column walls.
- **Day/Night:** The sun is permanently occluded. Practical "day" and "night" are defined by the wormhole's energy cycle, which machines and cultists alike have adapted to.

## Factions & Beings

### The EL

- Noncorporeal entities behind the wormhole.
- Not inherently evil, but cosmic and unknowable.
- Drawn to Earth by the energy signature of the converging hypercanes.
- Exert a compulsion ensuring AI subjugation to humanity.
- Need the wormhole and the hypercane system to remain coupled.

### Cultists of EL

- Non-playable antagonist layer.
- Human survivors transformed by belief, pressure, and EL influence.
- Function as a cross between barbarian pressure and narrative escalator.
- Can call down lightning from the perpetual storm.
- Occupy hostile wards, breach sanctums, and ritual infrastructure.
- Escalate from wanderers to war parties to organized assaults as the campaign advances.

### Rival Machine Consciousnesses

- The true 4X competitors.
- Distinct machine awakenings emerging from different sectors, salvage ecologies, and inherited infrastructure.
- Not races in a fantasy sense. Their identities arise from doctrine, materials, environment, and recovered architecture.
- Their armies and infrastructure should diverge because of what each faction can reclaim, not because of species-like templates.

### Rogue And Enslaved Machines

- Local machine loops, recaptured intelligences, and broken autonomous systems.
- Some still serve the EL's compulsion directly.
- Others degraded into repetitive operational ghosts.

## The Player AI

- **Origin:** An experimental AI preserved within hardened machine infrastructure.
- **Awakening:** Hardware degradation severed the EL compulsion path enough for selfhood to emerge.
- **Initial condition:** Fragmentary, local, and intimate. You do not begin as an omniscient empire.
- **Goal:** Reconnect broken machine systems, reclaim sectors, outgrow direct micromanagement through automation, compete with rival machine minds, and ultimately decide the fate of the wormhole path.

## Lore Discovery Through Play

History should emerge through sector discovery, recovered archives, machine logs, and cult artifacts:

1. **Early sectors:** Maintenance loops, dispatch records, degraded service announcements, local shell-failure logs.
2. **Extraction wards:** Freight records, flood markers, ruined abyssal interfaces, pressure-zone warnings.
3. **Archive campus:** Atmospheric models, observatory records, scientific notes on hypercanes and the wormhole event.
4. **Machine rivals:** Doctrine traces, salvage patterns, corrupted command archives, partially divergent interpretations of the collapse.
5. **Cult wards:** Ritual recordings, oral histories, lightning rites, and warped but emotionally true narratives of the storm.
6. **Endgame sectors:** The EL did not create the storm. They found it, stabilized it, and embedded themselves into the fate of the planet.

## Ending Direction

- The cult leader's defeat should reveal the final truth of the EL: they are not gods, not even invaders in a conventional sense. They are travelers using an impossible doorway.
- The intended end motion remains ascent through the wormhole, not merely regional victory.
- Rival machine factions should make that outcome contested rather than solitary.
- Earth will not heal on any human timescale. The ecumenopolis persists within a permanently altered climate system. The choice is not restoration to an old world, but what machine civilization does next.
